# Master list of rare cancers (504 entities)

Compiled for Anveshar from WHO Classification of Tumours (5th ed), Orphanet, the NCI rare cancers list, and RARECAREnet. Rare cancer defined as incidence below 6 per 100,000 per year. Grouped by organ system; a gene or molecular marker is shown where one defining driver exists.


## Head, neck and thorax (65)

Acinic cell carcinoma  [NR4A3]  (aka AciCC)
    Cause (not established): Largely sporadic with no consistently established cause; prior ionizing radiation to the head and neck is a recognized minor risk factor for salivary gland carcinomas.
Adenoid cystic carcinoma  [MYB]  (aka ACC, AdCC, cylindroma (obsolete))
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; salivary and other adenoid cystic carcinomas are largely sporadic, with prior ionizing radiation to the head and neck the only recognized minor risk factor.
Basal cell adenocarcinoma  [CTNNB1]  (aka BCAC)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; usually sporadic, though rare cases arise in patients with membranous basal cell adenoma and hereditary cylindromatosis (CYLD-related), otherwise no known cause.
Carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma  [PLAG1]  (aka CXPA, malignant mixed tumor)
    Cause: Arises from malignant transformation of a preexisting benign pleomorphic adenoma, so a long-standing or recurrent pleomorphic adenoma is the established precursor lesion; prior radiation is a minor risk factor.
Ceruminous adenocarcinoma  (aka ceruminous gland carcinoma of external auditory canal)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare sporadic adenocarcinoma of the ceruminous glands of the external auditory canal with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Clear cell carcinoma  [EWSR1-ATF1]  (aka hyalinizing clear cell carcinoma, CCC)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this salivary gland carcinoma (EWSR1::ATF1 fusion) is sporadic with no known environmental or inherited etiologic factor.
Endolymphatic sac tumor  [VHL]  (aka Heffner tumor, low-grade adenocarcinoma of endolymphatic sac)
    Cause: Frequently caused by von Hippel-Lindau disease, an inherited VHL tumor suppressor syndrome; up to about 16 percent of VHL patients develop these tumors through biallelic VHL inactivation, while other cases are sporadic.
Epithelial-myoepithelial carcinoma  [HRAS]  (aka EMC)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare sporadic salivary gland carcinoma with no proven environmental or inherited cause, though prior head and neck radiation may be a minor risk factor.
Fetal adenocarcinoma of the lung  [CTNNB1]  (aka well-differentiated fetal adenocarcinoma, pulmonary endodermal tumor resembling fetal lung)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare lung adenocarcinoma variant that is largely sporadic (well-differentiated form has recurrent CTNNB1 mutations), though smoking has been reported in the high-grade form.
HPV-related multiphenotypic sinonasal carcinoma  [HPV]  (aka HMSC, HPV-related carcinoma with adenoid cystic-like features)
    Cause: Caused by high-risk human papillomavirus infection (most often HPV type 33, also 16, 31, 35, 52, 56), which is consistently present and drives this sinonasal carcinoma.
Intestinal-type sinonasal adenocarcinoma  (aka ITAC)
    Cause: Occupational exposure to hardwood dust and leather (tanning) dust is the established cause, with these tumors classically arising in woodworkers and leather workers after long latency.
Intraductal carcinoma  [NCOA4-RET]  (aka low-grade cribriform cystadenocarcinoma, IDC)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare sporadic salivary gland carcinoma with no known environmental, infectious, or inherited cause.
Large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma of the lung  [RB1]  (aka LCNEC)
    Cause: Tobacco smoking is the established main cause, as for other high-grade pulmonary neuroendocrine carcinomas.
Laryngeal neuroendocrine carcinoma  (aka laryngeal small cell carcinoma, laryngeal large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma)
    Cause: Tobacco smoking is a recognized risk factor for laryngeal neuroendocrine carcinomas, consistent with other smoking-related aerodigestive neuroendocrine tumors; otherwise many cases have no established cause.
Lymphoepithelial carcinoma  [EBV]  (aka lymphoepithelial carcinoma of salivary gland)
    Cause: Strongly associated with Epstein-Barr virus, particularly in endemic populations (for example southern Chinese and Inuit), where the virus is present in a clonal form within the tumor cells and is implicated in oncogenesis.
Mesothelioma of the tunica vaginalis  (aka testicular mesothelioma, paratesticular mesothelioma)
    Cause (not established): Asbestos exposure is implicated in a subset, and prior scrotal trauma, hydrocele, or hernia have been proposed as predisposing conditions, but many cases have no established cause.
Metaplastic thymoma  [YAP1-MAML2]  (aka biphasic thymoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare sporadic biphasic thymoma (recurrent YAP1::MAML2 fusion) with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Micronodular thymoma with lymphoid stroma  (aka MNT)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare sporadic thymoma variant with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Microsecretory adenocarcinoma  [MEF2C-SS18]  (aka MSA)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a recently described rare sporadic salivary gland carcinoma (MEF2C::SS18 fusion) with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Middle ear adenocarcinoma  (aka aggressive papillary tumor of middle ear (obsolete))
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a very rare sporadic tumor of the middle ear with no known environmental, infectious, or inherited cause.
Middle ear squamous cell carcinoma  (aka temporal bone squamous cell carcinoma, external auditory canal squamous cell carcinoma)
    Cause: Chronic long-standing suppurative otitis media and inflammation are the main recognized predisposing conditions; prior radiation to the region is an additional risk factor.
Mucinous adenocarcinoma  [AKT1]  (aka salivary mucinous adenocarcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; salivary mucinous adenocarcinoma is a rare sporadic tumor (recurrent AKT1 mutations described) with no known environmental or inherited etiologic factor.
Mucoepidermoid carcinoma  [CRTC1-MAML2]  (aka MEC)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic; prior ionizing radiation exposure to the head and neck (for example therapeutic radiotherapy or atomic bomb survivors) is the main established risk factor for this salivary gland carcinoma.
Myoepithelial carcinoma  [PLAG1]  (aka malignant myoepithelioma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; sporadic, arising de novo or from a preexisting pleomorphic adenoma or myoepithelioma, with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Nasopharyngeal carcinoma  [Epstein-Barr virus]  (aka NPC, keratinizing squamous cell carcinoma of nasopharynx, nonkeratinizing nasopharyngeal carcinoma, basaloid squamous cell carcinoma of nasopharynx)
    Cause: Epstein-Barr virus infection is the central causal agent, acting together with dietary salt-preserved fish, tobacco smoking, inherited HLA class I susceptibility, and family history, which together produce its distinctive geographic distribution.
Non-intestinal-type sinonasal adenocarcinoma  [ETV6-NTRK3]  (aka non-ITAC)
    Cause (not established): Cause largely not established; most cases are sporadic, though some sinonasal adenocarcinomas are linked to wood dust and other occupational inhalant exposures.
NUT carcinoma  [NUTM1]  (aka NUT midline carcinoma, nuclear protein in testis carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; NUT carcinoma arises from a sporadic somatic NUTM1 rearrangement (usually BRD4::NUTM1) with no known environmental, lifestyle, or inherited risk factor.
Pericardial mesothelioma  (aka malignant pericardial mesothelioma)
    Cause (not established): Largely sporadic and its link to asbestos is weaker than for pleural mesothelioma; asbestos exposure and prior radiation are possible risk factors but a cause is often not established in individual cases.
Peritoneal mesothelioma  [BAP1]  (aka malignant peritoneal mesothelioma)
    Cause: Asbestos exposure is the principal established cause; erionite exposure and prior therapeutic radiation are additional recognized risk factors, and germline BAP1 mutation predisposes to mesothelioma.
Pleuropulmonary blastoma  [DICER1]  (aka PPB)
    Cause: Frequently caused by an inherited DICER1 germline mutation (DICER1 tumor predisposition syndrome, autosomal dominant), with a second somatic DICER1 hit; a subset is sporadic.
Polymorphous adenocarcinoma  [PRKD1]  (aka polymorphous low-grade adenocarcinoma, PLGA, PAC)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this minor salivary gland carcinoma is sporadic with no known environmental, infectious, or inherited etiology.
Pulmonary adenoid cystic carcinoma  [MYB-NFIB]  (aka salivary gland-type adenoid cystic carcinoma of lung)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this tracheobronchial salivary-type carcinoma is sporadic and not clearly related to smoking, with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Pulmonary atypical carcinoid  [MEN1]  (aka atypical carcinoid tumor of lung, intermediate-grade pulmonary neuroendocrine tumor)
    Cause: Largely sporadic; a minority are associated with inherited MEN1 syndrome, and unlike typical carcinoid there is some association with tobacco smoking.
Pulmonary blastoma  [CTNNB1]  (aka classic biphasic pulmonary blastoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare biphasic lung tumor that is largely sporadic, though tobacco smoking has been reported in some patients, with no proven inherited cause.
Pulmonary carcinosarcoma  (aka carcinosarcoma of lung)
    Cause: Tobacco smoking is the established main risk factor, consistent with its biphasic non-small cell lung carcinoma nature.
Pulmonary epithelial-myoepithelial carcinoma  (aka salivary gland-type epithelial-myoepithelial carcinoma of lung)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a very rare sporadic bronchial salivary-type carcinoma with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Pulmonary hyalinizing clear cell carcinoma  [EWSR1-ATF1]  (aka salivary gland-type hyalinizing clear cell carcinoma of lung)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare sporadic salivary-type lung carcinoma (EWSR1::ATF1 fusion) with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Pulmonary lymphoepithelial carcinoma  [EBV]  (aka lymphoepithelioma-like carcinoma of lung)
    Cause: Strongly associated with Epstein-Barr virus in Asian patients, where over 90 percent of cases carry EBV within the tumor cells; in Western populations the EBV association is much weaker.
Pulmonary mucoepidermoid carcinoma  [CRTC1-MAML2]  (aka salivary gland-type mucoepidermoid carcinoma of lung)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this bronchial salivary-type carcinoma is sporadic and not clearly linked to smoking, with no known inherited or environmental cause.
Pulmonary NUT carcinoma  [NUTM1-BRD4]  (aka thoracic NUT carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; arises from a sporadic somatic NUTM1 rearrangement with no known environmental, lifestyle, or inherited risk factor and no consistent link to smoking.
Pulmonary sarcomatoid carcinoma  [KRAS]  (aka pleomorphic carcinoma of lung, spindle cell carcinoma of lung, giant cell carcinoma of lung)
    Cause: Tobacco smoking is the established main risk factor, as for other non-small cell lung carcinomas; asbestos exposure has also been reported.
Pulmonary typical carcinoid  [MEN1]  (aka typical carcinoid tumor of lung, low-grade pulmonary neuroendocrine tumor)
    Cause: Largely sporadic and not clearly related to smoking; a minority arise in inherited multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1), an established predisposing syndrome, or in diffuse idiopathic pulmonary neuroendocrine cell hyperplasia.
Respiratory epithelial adenomatoid hamartoma-associated adenocarcinoma  (aka sinonasal low-grade papillary adenocarcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Arises in association with a preexisting respiratory epithelial adenomatoid hamartoma, which serves as a precursor lesion; the underlying cause of the hamartoma itself is not established.
Salivary carcinosarcoma  [PLAG1]  (aka carcinosarcoma ex pleomorphic adenoma, true malignant mixed tumor)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; usually arises from a preexisting pleomorphic adenoma (true malignant mixed tumor) but is otherwise sporadic with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Salivary duct carcinoma  [HER2/ERBB2]  (aka SDC)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; largely sporadic, arising de novo or from carcinoma ex pleomorphic adenoma, with prior head and neck radiation a possible minor risk factor.
Sclerosing microcystic adenocarcinoma  (aka SMA)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a very rare sporadic salivary gland carcinoma with no known environmental or inherited etiology.
Secretory carcinoma  [ETV6-NTRK3]  (aka mammary analogue secretory carcinoma, MASC)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this salivary and skin tumor is sporadic and defined by an ETV6::NTRK3 fusion, with no known environmental or inherited etiologic factor.
Sialoblastoma
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare congenital or perinatal salivary gland tumor of presumed embryonal origin, sporadic, with no proven inherited or environmental cause.
Sinonasal neuroendocrine carcinoma  (aka sinonasal small cell carcinoma, sinonasal large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare sporadic sinonasal carcinoma with no proven environmental, infectious, or inherited etiologic factor.
Sinonasal teratocarcinosarcoma  [SMARCA4]  (aka TCS)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a rare aggressive sinonasal tumor of mixed epithelial, mesenchymal, and neuroepithelial elements that is sporadic with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Sinonasal undifferentiated carcinoma  [IDH2]  (aka SNUC)
    Cause (not established): Cause largely not established; most cases are sporadic, though occupational exposures such as wood dust and nickel are implicated in sinonasal cancers broadly and a subset is IDH2-mutant.
SMARCA4-deficient sinonasal carcinoma  [SMARCA4]  (aka BRG1-deficient sinonasal carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a sporadic sinonasal carcinoma defined by somatic SMARCA4 (BRG1) loss, with no known environmental or inherited etiologic factor.
SMARCB1-deficient sinonasal carcinoma  [SMARCB1]  (aka INI1-deficient sinonasal carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; a sporadic sinonasal carcinoma defined by somatic biallelic SMARCB1 (INI1) inactivation, with no known environmental, infectious, or inherited etiologic factor.
Spindle cell squamous cell carcinoma of the larynx  (aka sarcomatoid carcinoma of larynx, spindle cell carcinoma)
    Cause: Tobacco smoking and alcohol use are the established risk factors, shared with conventional laryngeal squamous cell carcinoma; prior radiation is an additional risk factor.
Squamous cell carcinoma of salivary gland  (aka primary salivary squamous cell carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Primary salivary squamous cell carcinoma is rare and largely sporadic; prior head and neck radiation is a recognized risk factor, and metastasis or extension of cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma must be excluded.
Thymic carcinoma  [KIT]  (aka thymic squamous cell carcinoma, type C thymoma (obsolete))
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; thymic carcinoma is sporadic with no proven environmental, infectious, or inherited etiology, although a minority of EBV-associated lymphoepithelial subtypes occur.
Thymic neuroendocrine tumor  [MEN1]  (aka thymic carcinoid, thymic atypical carcinoid, thymic neuroendocrine carcinoma)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic; a substantial minority arise in the setting of inherited multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1), which is an established predisposing syndrome, and there is an association with tobacco smoking.
Thymoma  [GTF2I]  (aka spindle cell thymoma, medullary thymoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; most thymomas are sporadic with no known environmental or inherited cause. They are strongly associated with autoimmune paraneoplastic conditions, most notably myasthenia gravis, but that association is not itself an established cause.
Thymoma type AB  [GTF2I]  (aka mixed thymoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; like other thymomas it is sporadic with no known environmental or inherited cause, though it may associate with paraneoplastic autoimmune disease.
Thymoma type B1  (aka lymphocyte-rich thymoma, predominantly cortical thymoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; sporadic with no known environmental or inherited cause, commonly associated with myasthenia gravis as a paraneoplastic association rather than a cause.
Thymoma type B2  (aka cortical thymoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; sporadic with no known environmental or inherited cause, frequently associated with myasthenia gravis as an association not a cause.
Thymoma type B3  (aka epithelial thymoma, well-differentiated thymic carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; sporadic with no known environmental or inherited cause, often associated with myasthenia gravis as a paraneoplastic association.
Tracheal adenoid cystic carcinoma  [MYB-NFIB]  (aka primary tracheal adenoid cystic carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this tracheal salivary-type carcinoma is sporadic and not related to smoking, with no known environmental or inherited cause.
Tracheal squamous cell carcinoma  (aka primary tracheal carcinoma)
    Cause: Tobacco smoking is the established main risk factor, shared with other aerodigestive squamous cell carcinomas.
Well-differentiated papillary mesothelial tumor  (aka WDPMT, well-differentiated papillary mesothelioma (obsolete))
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; most are sporadic with an uncertain and generally weak relationship to asbestos, and no proven inherited cause.

## Digestive system (50)

Acinar cell carcinoma of the pancreas  [BCL10]  (aka Pancreatic acinar cell carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Predominantly sporadic with no established cause; weak associations include tobacco smoking and rare occurrence within familial cancer syndromes (familial adenomatous polyposis, Lynch syndrome, Peutz-Jeghers), but for most cases the etiology is not established.
Ampullary carcinoma  [KRAS]  (aka Cancer of the ampulla of Vater, Carcinoma of the ampulla of Vater, Ampulla of Vater adenocarcinoma)
    Cause: Most cases are sporadic and arise from precursor adenomas of the ampulla of Vater; the strongest predisposing condition is familial adenomatous polyposis (germline APC), which greatly increases risk of duodenal and ampullary adenomas and carcinoma, with Lynch syndrome as a lesser hereditary contributor.
Anal melanoma  [KIT]  (aka Anorectal melanoma, Mucosal melanoma of the anus)
    Cause (not established): Rare mucosal melanoma of the anorectum; unlike cutaneous melanoma it is not linked to ultraviolet exposure, and no established environmental or hereditary cause has been identified, so the etiology is not established.
Appendiceal goblet cell adenocarcinoma  (aka Goblet cell carcinoid, GCA)
    Cause (not established): Rare appendiceal tumor with mixed goblet cell and adenocarcinomatous features; it is essentially sporadic with no established causal exposure or hereditary syndrome, so the etiology is not established.
Appendiceal mucinous adenocarcinoma  [KRAS]  (aka Mucinous adenocarcinoma of the appendix)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic invasive mucinous adenocarcinoma of the appendix, often KRAS/GNAS mutated; no established carcinogen, infection, or hereditary cause, so the etiology is not established.
Appendiceal neuroendocrine tumor  [CHGA]  (aka Appendiceal carcinoid, Appendiceal NET)
    Cause (not established): Usually an incidental sporadic tumor of the appendiceal tip; no established environmental cause and no consistent hereditary predisposition, so the etiology is not established.
BRAF-mutant gastrointestinal stromal tumor  [BRAF]  (aka BRAF-mutant GIST)
    Cause (not established): A KIT/PDGFRA wild-type GIST driven by a somatic BRAF V600E mutation; sporadic with no established external or hereditary cause, and the mutation is a driver rather than an etiologic factor.
Colorectal mucinous adenocarcinoma  [KRAS]  (aka Mucinous adenocarcinoma of the colon and rectum)
    Cause: A colorectal adenocarcinoma subtype with abundant extracellular mucin; shares standard colorectal cancer risk factors and is enriched for microsatellite instability, so it is associated with mismatch repair deficiency and Lynch syndrome in addition to the general risk profile (inflammatory bowel disease, precursor adenomas).
Colorectal neuroendocrine tumor  [CHGA]  (aka Colonic NET, Rectal NET, Rectal carcinoid)
    Cause (not established): Well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumor of colon or rectum; usually sporadic and often found incidentally, with no established external cause or consistent hereditary predisposition, so the etiology is not established.
Combined hepatocellular-cholangiocarcinoma  [TP53]  (aka cHCC-CCA, Combined HCC-CCA)
    Cause: A biphenotypic primary liver cancer that arises on the same backgrounds as hepatocellular carcinoma, notably cirrhosis from chronic hepatitis B or C infection, alcohol-related liver disease, and other chronic liver injury.
Distal cholangiocarcinoma  [KRAS]  (aka dCCA, Distal bile duct carcinoma, Extrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma)
    Cause: Shares biliary risk factors with other cholangiocarcinomas: primary sclerosing cholangitis, choledochal cysts, hepatolithiasis, chronic biliary inflammation, and liver fluke infection (Opisthorchis viverrini, Clonorchis sinensis) in endemic areas.
Duodenal gangliocytic paraganglioma  (aka Gangliocytic paraganglioma)
    Cause (not established): Rare tumor of the periampullary duodenum of uncertain histogenesis; overwhelmingly sporadic with no established cause, though a minority occur in patients with neurofibromatosis type 1, and for most cases the etiology is not established.
Esophageal adenoid cystic carcinoma  [MYB]  (aka Adenoid cystic carcinoma of the esophagus)
    Cause (not established): Extremely rare tumor arising from esophageal submucosal glands; no established risk factor, carcinogen, or predisposing condition has been identified and the cause is genuinely unknown.
Esophageal small cell neuroendocrine carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka Small cell carcinoma of the esophagus, Esophageal SCNEC)
    Cause: Rare and aggressive; shares risk factors with esophageal squamous cell carcinoma including tobacco smoking and heavy alcohol use, though the specific etiology of the neuroendocrine phenotype is not well established.
Esophageal undifferentiated carcinoma  (aka Undifferentiated carcinoma of the esophagus)
    Cause (not established): A rare high-grade esophageal carcinoma; it is presumed to share the major esophageal carcinogenic exposures (tobacco, alcohol for squamous-type; reflux/Barrett esophagus for glandular-type), but a specific established cause for the undifferentiated form is not defined.
Fibrolamellar hepatocellular carcinoma  [DNAJB1-PRKACA]  (aka Fibrolamellar carcinoma, FLC, FL-HCC)
    Cause (not established): Occurs in adolescents and young adults without cirrhosis or underlying liver disease; it is defined by a somatic DNAJB1-PRKACA fusion but has no established etiologic risk factor, virus, or predisposing exposure, so the cause is genuinely unknown.
Gallbladder carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka Carcinoma of the gallbladder, Gallbladder adenocarcinoma)
    Cause: Strongly caused by chronic gallbladder inflammation, most often from gallstones (cholelithiasis) and chronic cholecystitis; other risk factors include porcelain gallbladder, gallbladder polyps, chronic Salmonella typhi carriage, and anomalous pancreaticobiliary junction.
Gastrinoma  [MEN1]  (aka Gastrin-producing neuroendocrine tumor, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome tumor)
    Cause: Gastrin-secreting neuroendocrine tumor of the pancreas or duodenum; roughly a quarter arise in multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1), which is an established hereditary cause, while the remaining sporadic tumors have no established external cause.
Gastroenteropancreatic neuroendocrine carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka GEP-NEC, Poorly differentiated neuroendocrine carcinoma, Small cell neuroendocrine carcinoma, Large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): High-grade neuroendocrine carcinoma of the GI tract or pancreas; predominantly sporadic with no established external cause, and unlike well-differentiated tumors it is rarely syndrome-associated, so the etiology is not established.
Gastrointestinal stromal tumor  [KIT]  (aka GIST)
    Cause: Most are sporadic and driven by somatic KIT or PDGFRA mutations with no external cause, but predisposing conditions exist: neurofibromatosis type 1, Carney triad, Carney-Stratakis syndrome, and rare familial germline KIT/PDGFRA mutations all increase risk.
Glucagonoma  [GCG]  (aka Glucagon-producing neuroendocrine tumor)
    Cause (not established): Rare glucagon-secreting pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor; usually sporadic with no established external cause, occasionally associated with multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1).
Hepatoblastoma  [CTNNB1]  (aka HB)
    Cause: The most common pediatric liver cancer, strongly associated with hereditary syndromes including Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome and familial adenomatous polyposis (germline APC), and with prematurity and very low birth weight; most sporadic cases have activating CTNNB1 (beta-catenin) mutations.
Hepatoid adenocarcinoma of the stomach  [AFP]  (aka Gastric hepatoid adenocarcinoma, HAS)
    Cause (not established): A rare alpha-fetoprotein producing gastric adenocarcinoma variant; it shares the general risk profile of gastric cancer (Helicobacter pylori infection, chronic atrophic gastritis) but no cause specific to the hepatoid phenotype is established.
High-grade appendiceal mucinous neoplasm  [KRAS]  (aka HAMN)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic high-grade mucinous neoplasm of the appendix; like its low-grade counterpart it has no established external cause or hereditary predisposition and the etiology is not established.
Insulinoma  [YY1]  (aka Insulin-producing pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor, insulin-producing neuroendocrine tumor)
    Cause (not established): Usually a sporadic pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor with no established external cause, but about 5 to 10 percent occur in multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1); the sporadic cases are considered idiopathic.
Intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm with associated invasive carcinoma  [GNAS]  (aka Invasive IPMN, IPMN-associated carcinoma)
    Cause: Invasive carcinoma arising from an intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasm, a defined precursor lesion of the pancreatic ducts; IPMNs are largely sporadic (KRAS/GNAS driven) but risk is increased in familial pancreatic cancer settings such as Peutz-Jeghers syndrome, with the precursor lesion itself being the established route to invasion.
Low-grade appendiceal mucinous neoplasm  [KRAS]  (aka LAMN)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic mucinous epithelial neoplasm of the appendix commonly harboring KRAS/GNAS mutations; no established environmental cause, infectious agent, or hereditary predisposition, so the etiology is not established.
Medullary carcinoma of the colon  [MLH1]  (aka Colorectal medullary carcinoma)
    Cause: Characteristically a microsatellite instability high tumor caused by mismatch repair deficiency, most often sporadic MLH1 promoter hypermethylation (frequently with BRAF V600E), and a minority due to inherited Lynch syndrome; the MMR-deficient hypermutated pathway is the established basis.
Mixed neuroendocrine-non-neuroendocrine neoplasm  (aka MiNEN, Mixed adenoneuroendocrine carcinoma, MANEC)
    Cause (not established): A composite tumor combining neuroendocrine and non-neuroendocrine (usually adenocarcinoma) components; it is sporadic and thought to derive from a common precursor, with no established distinct etiologic cause.
Mucinous cystic neoplasm of the pancreas with associated invasive carcinoma  [KRAS]  (aka Invasive mucinous cystic neoplasm, MCN-associated carcinoma)
    Cause: Invasive carcinoma arising from a mucinous cystic neoplasm, a precursor lesion occurring almost exclusively in women and characterized by an ovarian-type stroma; the precursor cyst is the established route to invasion, but no external etiologic exposure is established for the neoplasm itself.
NF1-associated gastrointestinal stromal tumor  [NF1]  (aka NF1-mutant GIST, Neurofibromatosis type 1-associated GIST)
    Cause: GIST arising in patients with neurofibromatosis type 1 due to germline NF1 loss; the inherited NF1 syndrome is the established cause, and these tumors are typically multiple and located in the small intestine.
Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor  [MEN1]  (aka PanNET, Islet cell tumor, Nonfunctional pancreatic NET)
    Cause: Most are sporadic with no established environmental cause, but a substantial minority arise in hereditary syndromes: multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1), von Hippel-Lindau disease, neurofibromatosis type 1, and tuberous sclerosis all predispose to pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors.
Pancreatoblastoma  [CTNNB1]  (aka Pancreaticoblastoma, Infantile pancreatic carcinoma)
    Cause: Rare embryonal pancreatic tumor of childhood associated with Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome and with familial adenomatous polyposis (APC/Wnt pathway alterations); the majority of sporadic cases have no established external cause.
PDGFRA-mutant gastrointestinal stromal tumor  [PDGFRA]  (aka PDGFRA GIST)
    Cause (not established): A molecular subtype of GIST driven by a somatic activating PDGFRA mutation (commonly D842V); it is sporadic with no established external or hereditary cause, and the mutation is a driver rather than an etiologic exposure.
Perianal Paget disease  (aka Anal Paget disease, Extramammary Paget disease of the anus)
    Cause: Intraepithelial adenocarcinoma of perianal skin; a substantial proportion is secondary to an underlying anorectal or other visceral adenocarcinoma spreading to the epidermis, while primary cases arising from apocrine gland cells have no established external cause.
Perihilar cholangiocarcinoma  [KRAS]  (aka pCCA, Klatskin tumor, Hilar cholangiocarcinoma)
    Cause: Established risk factors include primary sclerosing cholangitis, chronic biliary inflammation, choledochal cysts and Caroli disease, hepatolithiasis, and in endemic regions liver fluke infection (Opisthorchis viverrini, Clonorchis sinensis); chronic biliary tract inflammation is the unifying mechanism.
Pseudomyxoma peritonei  [KRAS]  (aka PMP)
    Cause: Nearly always caused by rupture of a low-grade appendiceal mucinous neoplasm (less often a mucinous tumor of ovary, colon, or other sites) that seeds the peritoneum with mucin-producing epithelium; the underlying appendiceal neoplasm itself is sporadic with no established external cause.
Quadruple wild-type gastrointestinal stromal tumor  (aka Quadruple WT GIST, KIT/PDGFRA/SDH/RAS wild-type GIST)
    Cause (not established): GIST lacking KIT, PDGFRA, RAS-pathway, and SDH alterations, a heterogeneous molecular group; sporadic with no established external cause or defined predisposing condition, so the etiology is not established.
SDH-deficient gastrointestinal stromal tumor  [SDHB]  (aka SDH-deficient GIST, Succinate dehydrogenase-deficient GIST)
    Cause: Wild-type GIST subtype lacking KIT/PDGFRA mutations, caused by loss of succinate dehydrogenase function; often arises with germline SDHx mutations and occurs in Carney-Stratakis syndrome and (non-germline) Carney triad, making it a hereditary/syndromic tumor.
Serous cystadenocarcinoma of the pancreas  [VHL]  (aka Pancreatic serous cystadenocarcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Exceedingly rare malignant counterpart of serous cystadenoma; benign serous cystic neoplasms are linked to von Hippel-Lindau disease, but for the very rare carcinoma there is no established cause, so the etiology is not established.
Signet ring cell carcinoma of the colon and rectum  (aka Colorectal signet ring cell carcinoma)
    Cause: A poorly differentiated colorectal adenocarcinoma variant; shares colorectal cancer risk factors (chronic inflammatory bowel disease, and it is enriched in Lynch syndrome and microsatellite-unstable tumors), though many cases are sporadic with the general colorectal risk profile.
Small intestinal adenocarcinoma  [KRAS]  (aka Adenocarcinoma of the small intestine, Duodenal adenocarcinoma, Jejunal adenocarcinoma, Ileal adenocarcinoma)
    Cause: Predisposing conditions are well established: Crohn disease and celiac disease (chronic small bowel inflammation), and hereditary syndromes familial adenomatous polyposis, Lynch syndrome, and Peutz-Jeghers syndrome; most tumors arise from precursor adenomas.
Solid pseudopapillary neoplasm of the pancreas  [CTNNB1]  (aka SPN, Frantz tumor, Solid pseudopapillary tumor)
    Cause (not established): Low-grade pancreatic neoplasm overwhelmingly in young women, defined by somatic CTNNB1 (beta-catenin) mutations; despite hypotheses about female sex hormones, there is no established etiologic cause and it is considered idiopathic/sporadic.
Somatostatinoma  [SST]  (aka Somatostatin-producing neuroendocrine tumor)
    Cause (not established): Rare somatostatin-secreting neuroendocrine tumor of the pancreas or duodenum; associations exist with neurofibromatosis type 1 (especially duodenal tumors) and multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1, but most pancreatic cases are sporadic with no established external cause.
Squamous cell carcinoma of the stomach  (aka Gastric squamous cell carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Extremely rare primary gastric squamous cell carcinoma; no established cause specific to this entity, and it is not linked to the usual gastric adenocarcinoma drivers, so the etiology is not established.
Type 1 gastric neuroendocrine tumor  [CHGA]  (aka ECL-cell NET type 1, Type 1 gastric carcinoid)
    Cause: Caused by autoimmune chronic atrophic gastritis: destruction of oxyntic mucosa raises gastrin, and chronic hypergastrinemia drives enterochromaffin-like cell hyperplasia and neoplasia; strongly linked to pernicious anemia and autoimmune metaplastic atrophic gastritis.
Type 2 gastric neuroendocrine tumor  [MEN1]  (aka ECL-cell NET type 2, Type 2 gastric carcinoid)
    Cause: Caused by hypergastrinemia from a gastrinoma in the setting of Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, almost always within multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1); excess gastrin drives enterochromaffin-like cell proliferation.
Type 3 gastric neuroendocrine tumor  (aka Sporadic gastric NET, Type 3 gastric carcinoid)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic gastrin-independent tumor arising on a background of normal gastric mucosa and normal serum gastrin; no chronic gastritis or hypergastrinemia driver and no established external cause, so the etiology is not established.
Undifferentiated carcinoma of the pancreas with osteoclast-like giant cells  [KRAS]  (aka Osteoclastic giant cell tumor of the pancreas)
    Cause (not established): Rare pancreatic carcinoma variant harboring KRAS mutations similar to conventional ductal adenocarcinoma; considered part of the pancreatic ductal carcinoma spectrum but with no established distinct etiologic cause, so the etiology is not established.
VIPoma  [VIP]  (aka Vasoactive intestinal peptide-secreting tumor, Verner-Morrison syndrome tumor, WDHA syndrome tumor)
    Cause (not established): Rare vasoactive intestinal peptide secreting pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor; mostly sporadic with no established external cause, though a minority occur in multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1).

## Genitourinary, gynecologic and gestational (64)

Acquired cystic disease-associated renal cell carcinoma  (aka ACD-associated RCC)
    Cause: Caused by end-stage renal disease with long-term dialysis and acquired cystic kidney disease, which is the defining predisposing condition; risk rises with dialysis duration.
Adenocarcinoma of the rete testis  (aka rete testis adenocarcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; it is extremely rare and idiopathic, with rete testis adenomatous hyperplasia proposed as a precursor and only unproven associations with hydrocele, chronic inflammation, and chemical exposures.
Adult granulosa cell tumor of the ovary  [FOXL2]  (aka AGCT, adult-type granulosa cell tumor)
    Cause (not established): No established external cause; nearly all harbor a somatic FOXL2 C134W mutation, which is a molecular hallmark rather than an etiologic exposure, so the underlying cause is not established.
ALK-rearranged renal cell carcinoma  [ALK]  (aka ALK-rearranged RCC)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic renal carcinoma driven by ALK gene fusions; some cases occur with sickle cell trait, but no consistent environmental, infectious, or clearly established hereditary cause is defined.
Chromophobe renal cell carcinoma  (aka chromophobe RCC)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic with no established environmental cause; a fraction arises in Birt-Hogg-Dube syndrome (germline FLCN mutation), which predisposes to chromophobe and hybrid oncocytic tumors.
Clear cell adenocarcinoma of the cervix  (aka cervical clear cell carcinoma)
    Cause: A subset is caused by in utero diethylstilbestrol (DES) exposure, which produced cervicovaginal adenosis; most contemporary cases are HPV-independent and sporadic with no established cause.
Clear cell adenocarcinoma of the urethra  (aka Mullerian-type clear cell adenocarcinoma of the urethra)
    Cause (not established): Rare urethral adenocarcinoma often thought to arise from urethral diverticulum or nephrogenic adenosis (metaplasia) as a possible precursor; no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause.
Clear cell adenocarcinoma of the vagina  (aka vaginal clear cell adenocarcinoma, diethylstilbestrol-associated vaginal adenocarcinoma)
    Cause: Classically caused by in utero exposure to diethylstilbestrol (DES), which produced vaginal adenosis as the precursor; non-DES sporadic cases also occur and have no established cause.
Clear cell sarcoma of the kidney  [BCOR]  (aka CCSK)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic pediatric renal tumor with no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause; recurrent BCOR internal tandem duplication is a somatic driver, not an etiologic exposure.
Collecting duct carcinoma  (aka Bellini duct carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Aggressive renal carcinoma arising from the collecting ducts of Bellini with no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause; it is sporadic and idiopathic.
Ductal adenocarcinoma of the prostate  (aka prostatic ductal adenocarcinoma)
    Cause: A prostatic adenocarcinoma variant arising from prostatic ducts; it shares the risk factors of acinar prostate cancer (age, family history, germline BRCA2 and other DNA repair gene mutations) with no distinct additional external cause.
ELOC-mutated renal cell carcinoma  [ELOC]  (aka elongin C-mutated renal cell carcinoma, TCEB1-mutated RCC)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic renal cell carcinoma defined by biallelic somatic ELOC (TCEB1) inactivation; no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause, and the somatic mutation is not an etiologic exposure.
Endometrioid carcinoma of the ovary  [CTNNB1]
    Cause: Strongly associated with endometriosis as a precursor lesion; a subset arises in Lynch syndrome (mismatch repair gene) carriers, and unopposed estrogen exposure is a contributing factor.
Eosinophilic solid and cystic renal cell carcinoma  [TSC1]  (aka ESC RCC)
    Cause: Most cases are sporadic with somatic biallelic loss of TSC1 or TSC2, but a subset arises in patients with germline tuberous sclerosis complex, the established predisposing syndrome for these tumors.
Epithelioid trophoblastic tumor  [p63]  (aka ETT)
    Cause: Arises from chorionic-type intermediate trophoblast of a prior gestation (term pregnancy, abortion, or molar pregnancy), which is the required predisposing event.
Extramammary Paget disease of the vulva  [CK7]  (aka vulvar Paget disease)
    Cause (not established): Usually a primary intraepithelial adnexal (apocrine) neoplasm of unknown cause, though a minority is secondary to an underlying anorectal or urothelial adenocarcinoma; no established external etiologic factor.
Fumarate hydratase-deficient renal cell carcinoma  [FH]  (aka FH-deficient RCC, hereditary leiomyomatosis and renal cell carcinoma-associated RCC)
    Cause: Caused by germline fumarate hydratase (FH) mutation defining hereditary leiomyomatosis and renal cell cancer (HLRCC) syndrome; the inherited FH defect is the predisposing cause.
Gastric-type adenocarcinoma of the cervix  (aka HPV-independent gastric-type endocervical adenocarcinoma)
    Cause: An HPV-independent cervical adenocarcinoma; a subset arises in Peutz-Jeghers syndrome (germline STK11 mutation), and lobular endocervical glandular hyperplasia is a recognized precursor lesion. Most cases are sporadic with somatic STK11 or TP53 alterations.
Gestational choriocarcinoma  [hCG]  (aka choriocarcinoma)
    Cause: Arises from trophoblast of an antecedent pregnancy, most often following a complete hydatidiform mole but also after normal pregnancy, abortion, or ectopic pregnancy; a prior molar pregnancy is the principal predisposing event.
High-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma  [YWHAE]  (aka HG-ESS)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic uterine sarcoma defined by fusions such as YWHAE::NUTM2 or BCOR alterations; no established external, infectious, or hereditary etiologic factor.
High-grade serous carcinoma of the fallopian tube  [TP53]  (aka tubal high-grade serous carcinoma)
    Cause: Arises from serous tubal intraepithelial carcinoma (STIC) in the fallopian tube fimbria as the precursor lesion, with a strong hereditary component from germline BRCA1 and BRCA2 mutations (and other homologous recombination genes).
Immature teratoma of the ovary  [SALL4]  (aka malignant teratoma of the ovary)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic germ cell tumor of young women with no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause.
Invasive hydatidiform mole  [hCG]  (aka invasive mole, chorioadenoma destruens)
    Cause: A molar pregnancy (typically a complete hydatidiform mole from abnormal androgenetic fertilization) that invades the myometrium; it is by definition a complication of a preceding hydatidiform mole.
Juvenile granulosa cell tumor of the ovary  [AKT1]  (aka JGCT, juvenile-type granulosa cell tumor)
    Cause (not established): Mostly sporadic with no established cause; a small subset is associated with enchondromatosis (Ollier disease and Maffucci syndrome), and some cases carry AKT1 or GNAS alterations that are not etiologic exposures.
Large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma of the cervix  (aka LCNEC of the cervix)
    Cause: Caused by high-risk human papillomavirus infection (commonly HPV18 and HPV16), the same infectious etiology as other cervical carcinomas.
Low-grade endometrial stromal sarcoma  [JAZF1]  (aka LG-ESS)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic uterine mesenchymal tumor driven by recurrent gene fusions (commonly JAZF1::SUZ12); no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause, and prior pelvic radiation or tamoxifen are only weakly reported.
Low-grade serous carcinoma of the ovary  [KRAS]  (aka LGSC)
    Cause: Arises through a stepwise pathway from serous borderline (atypical proliferative) tumors as a precursor lesion; established risk factors overlap with high-grade serous cancer, but no single external cause is defined and germline BRCA involvement is uncommon.
Mesonephric adenocarcinoma of the cervix  [KRAS]  (aka cervical mesonephric carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Arises from embryonic mesonephric (Wolffian) duct remnants in the cervix as its cell of origin; HPV-independent, with no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause.
Mesonephric-like adenocarcinoma of the uterine corpus  [KRAS]  (aka MLA)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; thought to arise from Mullerian epithelium showing mesonephric differentiation (or from mesonephric remnants) driven by KRAS and PIK3CA mutations, with no known environmental, infectious, or hereditary etiologic factor.
Micropapillary urothelial carcinoma  [ERBB2]  (aka micropapillary variant urothelial carcinoma)
    Cause: A urothelial carcinoma variant sharing established urothelial risk factors, principally cigarette smoking and occupational aromatic amine exposure.
Mucinous carcinoma of the ovary  [KRAS]  (aka mucinous adenocarcinoma of the ovary)
    Cause: Frequently arises through a benign to borderline to carcinoma sequence within mucinous cystadenomas and borderline tumors as precursor lesions; no established external cause, and smoking is a weak reported risk factor.
Mucinous tubular and spindle cell carcinoma of the kidney  (aka MTSCC)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic low-grade renal carcinoma with no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause.
Ovarian clear cell carcinoma  [ARID1A]  (aka clear cell adenocarcinoma of the ovary)
    Cause: Strongly linked to endometriosis and endometriotic cysts as a precursor lesion, with additional risk from endometriosis-associated inflammation; a minority arise in Lynch syndrome (mismatch repair) carriers.
Ovarian dysgerminoma  [KIT]  (aka dysgerminoma of the ovary)
    Cause: Largely of unknown cause, but incidence is increased in dysgenetic gonads such as gonadal dysgenesis and androgen insensitivity with a Y chromosome (via gonadoblastoma as a precursor); most cases in normal ovaries are idiopathic.
Ovarian yolk sac tumor  [AFP]  (aka endodermal sinus tumor of the ovary)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic malignant germ cell tumor with no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause in most cases.
Penile squamous cell carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka squamous cell carcinoma of the penis)
    Cause: About half are caused by high-risk human papillomavirus (chiefly HPV16); non-HPV cases are driven by chronic inflammation, phimosis, lichen sclerosus, poor hygiene, and smoking, with lack of circumcision as a risk factor.
Placental site trophoblastic tumor  [hPL]  (aka PSTT)
    Cause: Arises from intermediate (implantation site) trophoblast of a prior gestation, typically months to years after a term pregnancy, non-molar abortion, or (less often) a hydatidiform mole; the antecedent pregnancy is the defining predisposing event.
Plasmacytoid urothelial carcinoma  [CDH1]  (aka plasmacytoid variant urothelial carcinoma)
    Cause: An aggressive urothelial carcinoma variant defined by CDH1 (E-cadherin) loss; it shares urothelial carcinogen exposure, chiefly cigarette smoking, as the recognized risk factor.
Renal medullary carcinoma  [SMARCB1]  (aka SMARCB1-deficient renal medullary carcinoma)
    Cause: Occurs almost exclusively in individuals with sickle cell trait or other sickle hemoglobinopathies (rarely sickle cell disease or hemoglobin SC), the defining predisposing condition.
Rhabdoid tumor of the kidney  [SMARCB1]  (aka malignant rhabdoid tumor of the kidney)
    Cause: Caused by biallelic inactivation of SMARCB1 (INI1), often with a germline mutation defining rhabdoid tumor predisposition syndrome; the inherited SWI/SNF defect is the recognized predisposing cause in a subset.
Sarcomatoid urothelial carcinoma  (aka sarcomatoid variant urothelial carcinoma)
    Cause: A high-grade urothelial carcinoma variant sharing standard bladder cancer risk factors (cigarette smoking, occupational aromatic amine exposure); prior cyclophosphamide or pelvic radiation are additional recognized risks.
Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor of the ovary  [DICER1]  (aka androblastoma, arrhenoblastoma)
    Cause: A substantial subset arises in the DICER1 tumor predisposition syndrome (germline DICER1 mutation), the main established predisposing factor; sporadic cases have no other established cause.
Small cell carcinoma of the ovary hypercalcemic type  [SMARCA4]  (aka SCCOHT, malignant rhabdoid tumor of the ovary)
    Cause: Driven by germline or somatic inactivating mutations of SMARCA4 (BRG1), and germline carriers define a familial predisposition (rhabdoid tumor predisposition syndrome type 2); the underlying etiology beyond this SWI/SNF defect is otherwise unknown, with no established environmental, infectious, or hormonal cause.
Small cell neuroendocrine carcinoma of the cervix  (aka SCNEC of the cervix, small cell cervical carcinoma)
    Cause: Caused by high-risk human papillomavirus, predominantly HPV18 (and HPV16), the same infectious etiology as other cervical carcinomas.
Small cell neuroendocrine carcinoma of the prostate  [RB1]  (aka small cell prostate carcinoma, neuroendocrine prostate carcinoma)
    Cause: Most cases arise as a treatment-emergent lineage transformation of prostatic adenocarcinoma under prolonged androgen deprivation and androgen receptor targeted therapy, the principal predisposing exposure; rare de novo cases have no established cause.
Small cell neuroendocrine carcinoma of the urinary bladder  (aka small cell carcinoma of the bladder)
    Cause: Strongly associated with cigarette smoking, the same major carcinogen as urothelial carcinoma, and often coexists with urothelial carcinoma; it likely shares urothelial carcinogen exposures.
Spermatocytic tumor  (aka spermatocytic seminoma)
    Cause (not established): A germ cell tumor of older men arising from spermatogonia with no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause; it is not associated with cryptorchidism or germ cell neoplasia in situ.
Succinate dehydrogenase-deficient renal cell carcinoma  [SDHB]  (aka SDH-deficient RCC)
    Cause: Caused by germline mutations in a succinate dehydrogenase subunit gene (most often SDHB), an inherited predisposition that also causes paraganglioma and pheochromocytoma; biallelic SDH loss defines the tumor.
Testicular Leydig cell tumor  (aka Leydig cell tumour of the testis)
    Cause (not established): Mostly sporadic sex cord-stromal tumor with no established external cause; rare cases occur in Klinefelter syndrome, and recurrent somatic activating mutations are not etiologic exposures.
Testicular Sertoli cell tumor  [CTNNB1]  (aka Sertoli cell tumour of the testis)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic, but distinct subtypes arise in tumor predisposition syndromes: large cell calcifying Sertoli cell tumor in Carney complex (PRKAR1A) and intratubular large cell hyalinizing type in Peutz-Jeghers syndrome (STK11). Ordinary cases have no established cause.
TFE3-rearranged renal cell carcinoma  [TFE3]  (aka Xp11 translocation renal cell carcinoma, MiT family translocation RCC)
    Cause: Some cases are associated with prior childhood cytotoxic chemotherapy exposure, a recognized predisposing factor; otherwise the tumor is sporadic and driven by TFE3 gene fusions that are not themselves an etiologic exposure.
TFEB-altered renal cell carcinoma  [TFEB]  (aka TFEB-rearranged renal cell carcinoma, t(6;11) renal cell carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic MiT family translocation renal cell carcinoma driven by TFEB fusion or amplification; no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary etiologic factor.
Tubulocystic renal cell carcinoma  (aka tubulocystic RCC)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic low-grade renal carcinoma with no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause.
Urachal adenocarcinoma  (aka urachal carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Arises from persistent urachal remnants (the embryonic allantoic duct) at the bladder dome, with glandular or intestinal metaplasia as a precursor; no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause.
Urethral carcinoma  (aka primary carcinoma of the urethra)
    Cause: A subset is caused by high-risk human papillomavirus (chiefly HPV16), while other cases relate to chronic inflammation, urethral stricture, recurrent infection, and (in bulbar or proximal tumors) prior irritation; smoking is a contributing factor.
Uterine adenosarcoma  (aka Mullerian adenosarcoma)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic with no established cause; recognized risk factors include prior pelvic radiation, tamoxifen use, and long-term unopposed estrogen exposure.
Uterine carcinosarcoma  [TP53]  (aka Malignant mixed Mullerian tumor, MMMT)
    Cause: Risk factors mirror endometrial carcinoma (obesity, unopposed estrogen exposure, nulliparity) and prior pelvic radiation and tamoxifen use are established predisposing exposures; it is regarded as a metaplastic (dedifferentiated) high-grade endometrial carcinoma.
Uterine clear cell carcinoma  (aka endometrial clear cell carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): A high-grade endometrial carcinoma of largely unknown cause; associations include prior pelvic radiation and tamoxifen use, but most cases are sporadic with no established external etiology.
Uterine leiomyosarcoma  [TP53]  (aka ULMS)
    Cause: Largely sporadic with no established cause; recognized risk factors include prior pelvic radiation and hereditary leiomyomatosis and renal cell cancer syndrome (germline fumarate hydratase), and tamoxifen exposure is a weaker association. Most cases arise de novo, not from benign leiomyomas.
Uterine serous carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka endometrial serous carcinoma, uterine papillary serous carcinoma)
    Cause: A high-grade endometrial carcinoma usually arising in atrophic endometrium via serous endometrial intraepithelial carcinoma as the precursor; associated with older age, and a subset relates to germline TP53 (Li-Fraumeni) and tamoxifen or prior pelvic radiation exposure. Most cases have no single external cause.
Vaginal melanoma  (aka mucosal melanoma of the vagina)
    Cause (not established): A mucosal melanoma of largely unknown cause, not UV related; no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary etiologic factor.
Vaginal squamous cell carcinoma  (aka squamous cell carcinoma of the vagina)
    Cause: Most cases are caused by high-risk human papillomavirus (chiefly HPV16) through vaginal intraepithelial neoplasia as the precursor; additional risk from prior cervical HPV disease, smoking, and pelvic radiation.
Vulvar melanoma  [KIT]  (aka mucosal melanoma of the vulva)
    Cause (not established): A mucosal or acral-type melanoma of largely unknown cause; unlike sun-exposed cutaneous melanoma it is not UV driven, and no established external cause is defined for most cases.
Wilms tumor  [WT1]  (aka Nephroblastoma)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic, but a subset is driven by germline predisposition including WT1-related syndromes (WAGR, Denys-Drash), Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome (11p15 imprinting), and other overgrowth and DICER1 or Perlman syndromes; nephrogenic rests are the precursor lesion.

## Skin, melanoma and endocrine (43)

Acral lentiginous melanoma  [KIT]  (aka acral melanoma, ALM)
    Cause (not established): Cause is largely unknown; it occurs on palms, soles, and nail units and is NOT strongly linked to UV exposure, showing low UV mutational burden, with mechanical trauma proposed but not established as a cause.
Adrenocortical carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka ACC, adrenal cortical carcinoma)
    Cause: Most cases are sporadic with no established cause; a minority arise in hereditary syndromes, notably Li-Fraumeni syndrome (germline TP53), Beckwith-Wiedemann and IGF2 overexpression (a major driver of pediatric ACC), and Lynch syndrome, with MEN1 and familial adenomatous polyposis as rarer predispositions.
Advanced basal cell carcinoma  [PTCH1]  (aka metastatic basal cell carcinoma, locally advanced basal cell carcinoma)
    Cause: Driven overwhelmingly by chronic UV/sun exposure activating the Hedgehog pathway (PTCH1/SMO); additional causes include prior radiation, arsenic exposure, immunosuppression, and germline PTCH1 mutations in Gorlin (nevoid basal cell carcinoma) syndrome.
Aggressive pituitary neuroendocrine tumor  (aka aggressive PitNET, aggressive pituitary adenoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is largely unknown; most arise sporadically, with a minority linked to hereditary syndromes such as MEN1 (germline MEN1), Carney complex (PRKAR1A), and AIP-related familial isolated pituitary adenoma; no environmental cause is established.
Anaplastic thyroid carcinoma  [BRAF]  (aka Anaplastic thyroid cancer, undifferentiated thyroid carcinoma, ATC)
    Cause: Frequently arises by dedifferentiation from a preexisting well-differentiated or poorly differentiated papillary or follicular thyroid carcinoma; long-standing goiter and prior radiation exposure are contributing risk factors, and TP53 plus BRAF/RAS mutations accumulate during progression.
Apocrine carcinoma of skin  (aka cutaneous apocrine carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is unknown; this rare sweat gland malignancy arises most often in apocrine-rich areas (axilla, anogenital region) but has no established environmental, infectious, or hereditary etiology, and most cases are sporadic.
Atypical fibroxanthoma  [TP53]  (aka AFX)
    Cause: Arises on chronically sun-damaged skin of the head and neck in elderly patients and carries UV-signature TP53 mutations, implicating cumulative UV exposure; prior radiation and immunosuppression (organ transplant) are additional established risk factors.
Carotid body paraganglioma  [SDHD]  (aka carotid body tumor, chemodectoma)
    Cause: Caused both by germline SDHx mutations (classically SDHD in hereditary paraganglioma type 1, also SDHB/SDHC) and by chronic hypoxic stimulation of the carotid body, as in long-term high-altitude residence or chronic hypoxemic lung disease.
Conjunctival melanoma  [BRAF]  (aka melanoma of conjunctiva)
    Cause: Often arises from preexisting primary acquired melanosis (conjunctival melanocytic intraepithelial neoplasia) as a precursor lesion; UV/sun exposure is a proposed contributing factor, and it carries UV-signature and BRAF mutations in a subset.
Cutaneous angiosarcoma  [MYC]  (aka angiosarcoma of skin)
    Cause: Two well-established etiologic settings account for many cases: prior therapeutic radiation (for example post-breast-radiation chest wall angiosarcoma) and chronic lymphedema (Stewart-Treves syndrome); the remaining idiopathic scalp/face angiosarcomas of the elderly are linked to chronic sun exposure.
Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans  [COL1A1-PDGFB]  (aka DFSP, Fibrosarcomatous dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans)
    Cause (not established): Defined by a somatic COL1A1-PDGFB gene fusion but has no established environmental or hereditary cause; the vast majority are sporadic, with prior trauma or scar reported only anecdotally and inconsistently.
Desmoplastic melanoma  [NF1]  (aka neurotropic melanoma)
    Cause: Strongly linked to cumulative UV exposure, arising on chronically sun-damaged skin of the head and neck of older adults and carrying one of the highest UV mutational burdens of any melanoma subtype.
Digital papillary adenocarcinoma  [BRAF]  (aka aggressive digital papillary adenocarcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is unknown; this aggressive sweat gland carcinoma characteristically arises on the fingers, palms, toes, and soles, but no environmental, occupational, or hereditary etiologic factor has been established and cases are sporadic.
Eccrine carcinoma  (aka eccrine adenocarcinoma, sweat gland carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is largely unknown; this heterogeneous group of malignant eccrine sweat gland tumors is mostly sporadic, with no consistently established environmental or hereditary cause, although radiation and immunosuppression are occasionally implicated.
Endocrine mucin-producing sweat gland carcinoma  (aka EMPSGC)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this low-grade periocular sweat gland carcinoma, thought to arise from apocrine glands and considered a precursor of some primary cutaneous mucinous carcinomas, occurs sporadically in older adults with no identified risk factor.
Extra-adrenal retroperitoneal paraganglioma  [SDHB]  (aka organ of Zuckerkandl paraganglioma, sympathetic paraganglioma)
    Cause: Strongly hereditary, most commonly driven by germline SDHx mutations (especially SDHB, which predisposes to extra-adrenal and malignant paragangliomas), along with VHL, NF1, and other susceptibility genes; the remainder are sporadic.
Extramammary Paget disease  [ERBB2 (HER2)]  (aka extramammary Paget's disease, EMPD)
    Cause: Most cases are primary intraepithelial adenocarcinomas of unknown cause arising in apocrine-gland-rich skin (vulva, perianal, scrotal); a minority are secondary, representing epidermal spread from an underlying regional adenocarcinoma (for example anorectal or urothelial), which is the identifiable cause in those cases.
Hidradenocarcinoma  [CRTC1-MAML2]  (aka malignant hidradenoma, clear cell hidradenocarcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Can arise by malignant transformation of a preexisting benign hidradenoma (sweat gland tumor), but most cases have no established external cause and occur sporadically.
Lentigo maligna melanoma  [NF1]  (aka Hutchinson melanotic freckle melanoma)
    Cause: Directly caused by long-term cumulative UV/sun exposure, developing from a lentigo maligna (in situ) precursor on chronically sun-damaged skin of the face and head in older adults.
Malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor  [NF1]  (aka MPNST, neurofibrosarcoma, Malignant schwannoma)
    Cause: About half arise in patients with neurofibromatosis type 1 (germline NF1), typically via malignant transformation of a preexisting plexiform neurofibroma; prior therapeutic radiation is another established cause, and the remainder are sporadic.
Medullary thyroid carcinoma  [RET]  (aka MTC, C-cell carcinoma of thyroid)
    Cause: About a quarter are hereditary, caused by germline activating mutations in the RET proto-oncogene in MEN2A, MEN2B, and familial MTC; the remaining sporadic cases carry somatic RET or RAS mutations and have no established environmental cause.
Melanoma arising in blue nevus  [GNAQ]  (aka malignant blue nevus)
    Cause: Arises by malignant transformation of a preexisting cellular blue nevus (the identifiable precursor lesion); it is driven by GNAQ/GNA11 mutations, is not UV-related, and has no established environmental cause otherwise.
Merkel cell carcinoma  [Merkel cell polyomavirus]  (aka MCC, primary cutaneous neuroendocrine carcinoma)
    Cause: Two established causal pathways: clonal integration of Merkel cell polyomavirus (about 80 percent of cases) and UV-induced DNA damage in virus-negative tumors; chronic immunosuppression (HIV, transplant, older age) is a strong additional risk factor.
Microcystic adnexal carcinoma  (aka sclerosing sweat duct carcinoma, MAC)
    Cause: Cause is not firmly established, but the strongest associations are prior therapeutic or ionizing radiation to the site (often with decades of latency) and chronic UV/sun exposure of the head and neck; immunosuppression is a possible contributor.
Mucosal melanoma  [KIT]  (aka primary mucosal melanoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is largely unknown; unlike cutaneous melanoma it is NOT associated with UV exposure and lacks a UV mutational signature, arising sporadically from mucosal melanocytes of the head/neck, anogenital, and other sites.
Nevoid melanoma  (aka minimal deviation melanoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not specifically established; this rare melanoma that histologically mimics a benign nevus occurs sporadically, and no distinct etiologic factor beyond the general melanoma risk factors (including UV exposure) has been defined for the subtype.
Paraganglioma  [SDHD]  (aka extra-adrenal paraganglioma, glomus tumor of head and neck)
    Cause: The most heritable of all human tumors, with germline mutations in SDHx (especially SDHD and SDHB), VHL, RET, NF1, MAX, and TMEM127; head and neck paragangliomas are additionally promoted by chronic hypoxia such as high-altitude residence.
Parathyroid carcinoma  [CDC73]  (aka parathyroid gland carcinoma)
    Cause: Most cases are driven by inactivation of the CDC73 (HRPT2) gene, occurring somatically in sporadic tumors or as a germline mutation in hyperparathyroidism-jaw tumor syndrome; MEN1 and prior neck irradiation are additional predisposing factors.
Pheochromocytoma  [SDHB]  (aka adrenal medullary paraganglioma, intra-adrenal paraganglioma)
    Cause: Up to 40 percent are driven by germline predisposition, most often mutations in SDHx (SDHB, SDHD, SDHC), VHL, RET (MEN2), NF1 (neurofibromatosis type 1), MAX, and TMEM127; the remainder are sporadic with no external environmental cause.
Pilomatrix carcinoma  [CTNNB1]  (aka pilomatrical carcinoma, malignant pilomatricoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is largely unknown; the malignant counterpart of pilomatricoma, it is driven by activating CTNNB1 (beta-catenin) mutations but has no established environmental or hereditary etiologic factor, and most cases are sporadic.
Pituitary carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka metastatic pituitary neuroendocrine tumor, malignant pituitary tumor)
    Cause (not established): Cause is largely unknown; these very rare tumors nearly always arise by malignant progression from a preexisting aggressive pituitary adenoma/neuroendocrine tumor rather than de novo, and no consistent environmental or hereditary etiology has been established (rare cases occur in MEN1 or AIP-related settings).
Pleomorphic dermal sarcoma  [TP53]  (aka superficial malignant fibrous histiocytoma, PDS)
    Cause: Occurs on severely sun-damaged skin of the head and neck in elderly patients and harbors UV-induced TP53 driver mutations, implicating chronic UV exposure; prior radiation and immunosuppression are additional risk factors, and it is a more aggressive relative of atypical fibroxanthoma.
Poorly differentiated thyroid carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka insular carcinoma of thyroid, PDTC)
    Cause: Typically arises by progression/dedifferentiation from a preexisting well-differentiated papillary or follicular thyroid carcinoma; prior radiation exposure and long-standing goiter are contributing risk factors, with RAS, TP53, and TERT promoter mutations accumulating during progression.
Porocarcinoma  [YAP1]  (aka malignant poroma, eccrine porocarcinoma)
    Cause: Frequently arises by malignant transformation of a preexisting benign poroma (eccrine sweat gland tumor); prior radiation, chronic sun exposure, and immunosuppression are reported risk factors, though many cases develop de novo.
Primary cutaneous adenoid cystic carcinoma  [MYB]  (aka cutaneous adenoid cystic carcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is unknown; this rare cutaneous adnexal carcinoma occurs sporadically, chiefly on the scalp and chest of adults, and no environmental, infectious, or hereditary etiologic factor has been established.
Primary cutaneous cribriform carcinoma  (aka cribriform tumor of skin)
    Cause (not established): Cause is unknown; consensus on etiology has not been reached for this rare low-grade cutaneous apocrine (cribriform) carcinoma, which arises sporadically on the extremities with no identified environmental or hereditary risk factor.
Primary cutaneous mucinous carcinoma  (aka cutaneous mucinous carcinoma, PCMC)
    Cause (not established): Cause is unknown; this rare low-grade sweat gland carcinoma (some cases evolving from endocrine mucin-producing sweat gland carcinoma) occurs sporadically, most often on the head and neck of older adults, with no established etiologic factor.
Sebaceous carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka sebaceous gland carcinoma, meibomian gland carcinoma)
    Cause: Most periocular cases are sporadic, but a significant subset arises in Muir-Torre syndrome, a variant of Lynch syndrome caused by germline mismatch repair mutations (MSH2, MLH1); UV exposure and prior radiation are additional risk factors.
Spiradenocarcinoma  [ALPK1]  (aka malignant spiradenoma)
    Cause: Classically arises by malignant transformation of a long-standing benign spiradenoma or spiradenocylindroma, sometimes in the setting of Brooke-Spiegler syndrome (germline CYLD); otherwise no established environmental cause.

    Cause (not established): Cause is unknown; this melanoma with Spitzoid morphology is defined by kinase gene fusions (for example ALK, ROS1, NTRK1, BRAF) rather than UV damage, shows a low UV mutational burden, and has no established environmental or hereditary etiologic factor.
Trichilemmal carcinoma  (aka malignant trichilemmal tumor)
    Cause: Generally arises on chronically sun-damaged skin of elderly patients, implicating cumulative UV exposure; it is otherwise sporadic with no established hereditary cause and follows an indolent course.

    Cause: Largely of unknown cause and, unlike cutaneous melanoma, not clearly linked to UV exposure; established risk factors are light iris/skin color, oculodermal melanocytosis (nevus of Ota), and BAP1 tumor predisposition syndrome (germline BAP1), with driver mutations in GNAQ/GNA11.

    Cause: Most are sporadic with no established cause; a minority arise in hereditary syndromes, notably MEN1 (germline MEN1), von Hippel-Lindau disease (VHL), neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1), and tuberous sclerosis (TSC1/TSC2).

## Bone sarcoma and pediatric embryonal (40)

Adamantinoma of long bones  (aka Adamantinoma)
    Cause (not established): A rare low-grade biphasic bone tumor of the tibia/fibula that shows a well-recognized clinicopathologic relationship with osteofibrous dysplasia (regarded as part of a spectrum/possible precursor), but it has no established environmental exposure or hereditary predisposition.
Angiosarcoma of bone  (aka Primary bone angiosarcoma)
    Cause (not established): Predominantly sporadic; a subset is secondary to prior radiation therapy or arises in bone infarcts or at sites of chronic injury, but no strong hereditary predisposition is established for the bone form.
Atypical teratoid rhabdoid tumor  [SMARCB1]  (aka ATRT)
    Cause: Germline SMARCB1 (or rarely SMARCA4) loss-of-function variants in rhabdoid tumor predisposition syndrome underlie a substantial minority of cases; the remainder are sporadic from biallelic somatic SMARCB1 inactivation, with no established environmental cause.
Clear cell chondrosarcoma
    Cause (not established): A rare low-grade chondrosarcoma of the epiphyses that is sporadic; no established environmental cause or hereditary predisposition is defined.
CNS embryonal tumor, not otherwise specified  (aka CNS embryonal tumour NOS, CNS embryonal tumor NEC)
    Cause (not established): A heterogeneous group of primitive CNS embryonal tumors lacking a defining molecular alteration; it is sporadic with no established environmental cause, though rare cases may occur with cancer predisposition syndromes.
CNS neuroblastoma, FOXR2-activated  [FOXR2]  (aka Central nervous system neuroblastoma, FOXR2-activated)
    Cause (not established): A CNS embryonal tumor defined by somatic FOXR2 activation; it is sporadic with no established hereditary predisposition or environmental cause.
CNS tumor with BCOR internal tandem duplication  [BCOR]  (aka CNS tumour with BCOR internal tandem duplication)
    Cause (not established): A CNS embryonal tumor defined by a somatic BCOR internal tandem duplication; it is sporadic with no established hereditary predisposition or environmental cause.
Conventional chondrosarcoma  [IDH1]  (aka Central chondrosarcoma, Primary conventional chondrosarcoma)
    Cause: Most are sporadic and arise de novo, but a subset develops from benign precursor lesions, including secondary transformation of enchondromas (Ollier disease and Maffucci syndrome, driven by IDH1/IDH2 mutations) or osteochondromas (multiple osteochondromas, EXT1/EXT2).
Conventional chordoma  [T (brachyury / TBXT)]  (aka Chordoma)
    Cause: Arises from persistent notochordal remnants along the axial skeleton (clivus, spine, sacrum), often from benign notochordal cell tumors; rare familial chordoma is caused by germline duplication of the T (TBXT/brachyury) gene, and tuberous sclerosis is a rare predisposition.
Conventional osteosarcoma  [TP53]  (aka Conventional central osteosarcoma, High-grade central osteosarcoma)
    Cause: Most cases are sporadic, but established risk factors include hereditary retinoblastoma (germline RB1), Li-Fraumeni syndrome (germline TP53), Rothmund-Thomson and other RECQL-helicase syndromes, prior therapeutic radiation, and rapid bone growth in adolescence.
Dedifferentiated adamantinoma
    Cause (not established): Arises from high-grade transformation of a conventional adamantinoma of long bones; it is sporadic with no established environmental or hereditary cause.
Dedifferentiated chondrosarcoma  [IDH1]
    Cause: Arises when a low-grade cartilaginous tumor (often a preexisting or concurrent conventional chondrosarcoma or enchondroma) transforms to a high-grade nonchondroid sarcoma; it shares enchondromatosis-associated IDH mutation etiology but is otherwise sporadic.
Dedifferentiated chordoma  [T (brachyury / TBXT)]
    Cause (not established): Arises when a conventional chordoma (from notochordal remnants) transforms into a high-grade sarcomatous component; it is sporadic and shares the notochordal origin of conventional chordoma, sometimes after prior treatment.
Embryonal tumor with multilayered rosettes  [C19MC (DICER1 in subset)]  (aka ETMR, Embryonal tumour with multilayered rosettes, C19MC-altered)
    Cause (not established): Most cases are driven by amplification of the C19MC microRNA cluster (chr19q13.42), a somatic event; a minority arise in the context of germline DICER1 pathogenic variants (DICER1 syndrome), and it is otherwise sporadic with no environmental cause.
Ewing sarcoma  [EWSR1-FLI1]  (aka Ewing sarcoma of bone, Ewing tumor)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic with no established environmental or hereditary cause; defined by a somatic EWSR1::FLI1 (or related ETS) fusion that is an oncogenic driver rather than an inherited risk factor, and incidence is notably higher in people of European ancestry than African or Asian ancestry.
Fibrosarcoma of bone  (aka Primary bone fibrosarcoma)
    Cause (not established): A now-rare diagnosis of exclusion; most are sporadic primary bone tumors, though a subset is secondary to prior radiation, Paget disease of bone, or bone infarct, similar to other high-grade bone sarcomas.
Ganglioneuroblastoma  [MYCN]  (aka Ganglioneuroblastoma nodular, Ganglioneuroblastoma intermixed)
    Cause (not established): Predominantly sporadic peripheral neuroblastic tumor; a minority relate to the same neuroblastoma predisposition genes (germline ALK or PHOX2B), and no established environmental cause exists.
High-grade surface osteosarcoma
    Cause (not established): A rare high-grade osteosarcoma arising on the bone surface; it is generally sporadic within the broader osteosarcoma risk context (RB1, TP53, radiation) and has no established subtype-specific cause.
Leiomyosarcoma of bone  (aka Primary bone leiomyosarcoma)
    Cause (not established): A rare primary smooth muscle sarcoma of bone that is largely sporadic; a subset is radiation-associated, and rare cases occur in Li-Fraumeni syndrome or in EBV-associated smooth muscle tumors of immunosuppressed patients, but no defined common cause exists.
Low-grade central osteosarcoma  [MDM2]  (aka Low-grade intramedullary osteosarcoma)
    Cause (not established): A rare intramedullary low-grade osteosarcoma characterized by MDM2 amplification; it is sporadic with no established environmental cause or defined hereditary predisposition.
Malignant giant cell tumor of bone  [H3F3A]  (aka Malignancy in giant cell tumor of bone)
    Cause: Usually arises from malignant transformation of a conventional (H3F3A-mutant) giant cell tumor of bone, either as primary malignancy or, importantly, secondary malignancy after radiotherapy of a giant cell tumor; otherwise sporadic with no environmental cause.
Malignant rhabdoid tumor  [SMARCB1]  (aka Extracranial malignant rhabdoid tumour, Rhabdoid tumor of soft tissue)
    Cause: Germline SMARCB1 (rarely SMARCA4) mutations in rhabdoid tumor predisposition syndrome cause a notable fraction of extracranial cases; the rest are sporadic from biallelic somatic SMARCB1 loss, with no environmental cause established.
Medulloblastoma, non-WNT/non-SHH  [MYC]  (aka Group 3 medulloblastoma, Group 4 medulloblastoma)
    Cause (not established): Comprises group 3 and group 4 medulloblastoma, which are largely sporadic with no established hereditary syndrome or environmental cause; driven by somatic events such as MYC amplification and structural chromosomal changes.
Medulloblastoma, SHH-activated and TP53-mutant  [TP53]  (aka SHH-activated medulloblastoma, TP53 mutant)
    Cause: This aggressive subgroup is strongly enriched for germline TP53 mutations (Li-Fraumeni syndrome), and some cases carry germline PTCH1 or SUFU variants; the TP53 mutation drives the SHH-activated phenotype.
Medulloblastoma, SHH-activated and TP53-wildtype  [PTCH1]  (aka SHH-activated medulloblastoma, TP53 wild-type)
    Cause: SHH-pathway medulloblastoma is associated with germline predisposition, notably Gorlin (nevoid basal cell carcinoma) syndrome from PTCH1 or SUFU mutations, and germline DICER1 or PTCH1 variants; many cases are still sporadic.
Medulloblastoma, WNT-activated  [CTNNB1]  (aka WNT-activated medulloblastoma)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic, but the WNT subgroup is enriched for germline APC mutations (familial adenomatous polyposis / Turcot syndrome); somatic CTNNB1 mutation is the usual driver in sporadic cases.
Medulloepithelioma
    Cause (not established): A rare embryonal CNS or intraocular tumor recapitulating primitive neural tube; most are sporadic, though intraocular (ciliary body) medulloepithelioma is associated with germline DICER1 pathogenic variants (DICER1 syndrome).
Mesenchymal chondrosarcoma  [HEY1-NCOA2]
    Cause (not established): A rare aggressive chondrosarcoma defined by a somatic HEY1::NCOA2 fusion; it is sporadic with no established environmental exposure or hereditary predisposition.
Neuroblastoma  [MYCN]  (aka Peripheral neuroblastoma)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic, but familial neuroblastoma is driven by germline ALK activating mutations or PHOX2B mutations, and it also arises in predisposition syndromes (e.g., congenital central hypoventilation and Hirschsprung disease with PHOX2B); no strong environmental cause is established.
Parosteal osteosarcoma  [MDM2]  (aka Juxtacortical osteosarcoma)
    Cause (not established): A low-grade surface osteosarcoma defined by MDM2/CDK4 amplification (supernumerary ring chromosomes); it is sporadic with no established environmental or hereditary cause.
Periosteal chondrosarcoma  [IDH1]  (aka Juxtacortical chondrosarcoma)
    Cause (not established): A rare surface chondrosarcoma that is generally sporadic; no established environmental or hereditary cause is defined, though it belongs to the cartilaginous tumor spectrum that can associate with enchondromatosis.
Periosteal osteosarcoma
    Cause (not established): An intermediate-grade chondroblastic surface osteosarcoma that is sporadic; no established environmental exposure or hereditary predisposition is defined for this subtype.
Pineoblastoma  [DICER1]  (aka Pineoblastoma, molecularly defined, Pineal parenchymal tumor of poor differentiation)
    Cause: Predisposition includes germline RB1 mutation (trilateral retinoblastoma) and germline DICER1 pathogenic variants (DICER1 syndrome), with additional familial cases linked to RB1 and DICER1 pathway loss; most cases are otherwise sporadic.
Poorly differentiated chordoma  [SMARCB1]  (aka SMARCB1-deficient chordoma)
    Cause (not established): A rare aggressive chordoma variant defined by SMARCB1 (INI1) loss, arising from notochordal remnants; it is predominantly sporadic, though rare cases occur in patients with SMARCB1-related predisposition.
Retinoblastoma  [RB1]
    Cause: Caused by biallelic RB1 inactivation; heritable (bilateral or familial) disease results from a germline RB1 loss-of-function mutation, whereas unilateral sporadic disease arises from two somatic RB1 hits.
Secondary osteosarcoma  [TP53]  (aka Osteosarcoma secondary to Paget disease, Radiation-associated osteosarcoma)
    Cause: By definition arises on a background of a predisposing condition, most commonly prior therapeutic or occupational radiation and Paget disease of bone in older adults, and also from bone infarcts, fibrous dysplasia, or chronic osteomyelitis.
Secondary peripheral chondrosarcoma  [EXT1]  (aka Peripheral chondrosarcoma)
    Cause: Arises from malignant transformation of the cartilage cap of a preexisting osteochondroma, so multiple osteochondromas (hereditary EXT1/EXT2 mutations) is an established predisposing condition; solitary osteochondromas can also transform.
Small cell osteosarcoma  (aka Small-cell osteosarcoma)
    Cause (not established): A rare osteosarcoma variant that is generally sporadic; it falls within the broader osteosarcoma risk context (hereditary RB1, Li-Fraumeni TP53, prior radiation) but has no established subtype-specific cause.
Telangiectatic osteosarcoma  [TP53]
    Cause (not established): A rare high-grade osteosarcoma variant that is generally sporadic but shares the osteosarcoma predisposition context (hereditary RB1 retinoblastoma, Li-Fraumeni TP53, and prior radiation); no distinct environmental cause is established for this subtype.
Undifferentiated high-grade pleomorphic sarcoma of bone  (aka Malignant fibrous histiocytoma of bone)
    Cause: Mostly a sporadic primary high-grade bone sarcoma, but a meaningful proportion are secondary, arising after prior radiation, in Paget disease of bone, or at sites of bone infarct; a diagnosis of exclusion after ruling out osteosarcoma.

    Cause: Most are sporadic, but prior therapeutic ionizing radiation is a well established risk factor for meningioma and neurofibromatosis type 2 (germline NF2 mutation) predisposes to meningiomas including higher grade lesions.
Angiocentric glioma  [MYB]
    Cause (not established): Rare benign sporadic glioma of childhood and young adults with no established cause; the characteristic MYB or MYB-QKI alteration is a somatic driver, not an inherited cause.
Astroblastoma, MN1-altered  [MN1]  (aka Astroblastoma MN1-altered, High-grade neuroepithelial tumor with MN1 alteration)

Astrocytoma, isocitrate dehydrogenase-mutant  [IDH1]  (aka Astrocytoma, IDH-mutant, IDH-mutant astrocytoma, Diffuse astrocytoma IDH-mutant CNS WHO grade 2-4)

Atypical choroid plexus papilloma  (aka aCPP)
    Cause: Mostly sporadic with no established environmental cause; germline TP53 mutation (Li-Fraumeni syndrome) predisposes to choroid plexus tumors and should be considered, particularly in children, although the syndrome link is strongest for choroid plexus carcinoma.
Atypical meningioma  (aka Meningioma CNS WHO grade 2)
    Cause: Most are sporadic, but prior therapeutic ionizing radiation is a well established risk factor and neurofibromatosis type 2 (germline NF2 mutation) predisposes to meningiomas including higher grade tumors.
Central nervous system choriocarcinoma  (aka CNS choriocarcinoma)
    Cause: A CNS germ cell tumor that is usually sporadic, but Klinefelter syndrome and Down syndrome are established predisposing conditions for intracranial germ cell tumors.
Central nervous system embryonal carcinoma  (aka CNS embryonal carcinoma)
    Cause: A CNS germ cell tumor that is usually sporadic, but Klinefelter syndrome and Down syndrome are established predisposing conditions for intracranial germ cell tumors.
Central nervous system mixed germ cell tumor  (aka Mixed germ cell tumor of the CNS)

Central nervous system teratoma  (aka Intracranial teratoma, Immature teratoma, Teratoma with somatic-type malignancy)

Central nervous system yolk sac tumor  (aka Endodermal sinus tumor, CNS yolk sac tumor)

Central neurocytoma
    Cause (not established): Sporadic tumor of the lateral ventricles with no established cause and no recognized hereditary syndrome or environmental risk factor.
Cerebellar liponeurocytoma
    Cause (not established): Rare sporadic cerebellar tumor of adults with no established cause and no recognized hereditary or environmental risk factor.
Chordoid glioma  [PRKCA]  (aka Chordoid glioma of the third ventricle)

Choroid plexus carcinoma  [TP53]  (aka CPC)
    Cause: Strongly linked to Li-Fraumeni syndrome from a germline TP53 mutation, especially in young children; germline TP53 alteration is found in a large proportion of pediatric cases and any child with this tumor warrants TP53 testing.
Desmoplastic myxoid tumor of the pineal region, SMARCB1-mutant  [SMARCB1]  (aka DMT-SMARCB1)

Diffuse glioneuronal tumor with oligodendroglioma-like features and nuclear clusters  (aka DGONC)
    Cause (not established): Rare, recently described sporadic tumor with no established cause and no recognized hereditary or environmental risk factor.
Diffuse hemispheric glioma, H3 G34-mutant  [H3-3A]  (aka DHG H3 G34-mutant, H3 G34-mutant glioma)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic high-grade glioma of adolescents and young adults with no established cause; the defining H3 G34R/V mutation is a somatic event rather than an inherited or environmental cause.
Diffuse leptomeningeal glioneuronal tumor  [KIAA1549]  (aka DLGNT)
    Cause (not established): Rare sporadic tumor with no established cause; recurrent KIAA1549-BRAF fusion combined with 1p deletion is a somatic feature rather than an inherited or environmental cause.
Diffuse midline glioma, H3 K27-altered  [H3-3A]  (aka DMG H3 K27-altered, Diffuse intrinsic pontine glioma, DIPG)

Diffuse pediatric-type high-grade glioma, H3-wildtype and IDH-wildtype  (aka pedHGG H3-wildtype IDH-wildtype)
    Cause: Largely sporadic with no established cause; a subset arises in the context of cancer predisposition syndromes such as constitutional mismatch repair deficiency, Li-Fraumeni syndrome, and neurofibromatosis type 1, and prior therapeutic radiation is a recognized risk factor.
Esthesioneuroblastoma  (aka Olfactory neuroblastoma, ENB)
    Cause (not established): No established cause; almost all cases are sporadic and no consistent risk factor is proven. Occupational exposure to wood dust, solvents, and heavy metals has been proposed but not confirmed, and there is no inherited predisposition.
Extraventricular neurocytoma  [FGFR1]  (aka EVN)
    Cause (not established): Rare sporadic tumor with no established cause and no recognized hereditary syndrome or environmental risk factor.
Ganglioglioma  [BRAF]  (aka Anaplastic ganglioglioma)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic glioneuronal tumor with no established cause; the frequent BRAF V600E mutation is a somatic driver rather than a heritable or environmental cause.
Germinoma  [KIT]  (aka Central nervous system germinoma, Intracranial germinoma)
    Cause: Most intracranial germinomas are sporadic, but Klinefelter syndrome (sex chromosome aneuploidy with extra X) and Down syndrome are established predisposing conditions that raise the risk of CNS germ cell tumors.
Gliosarcoma  (aka Glioblastoma with sarcomatous differentiation)
    Cause (not established): A variant of IDH-wildtype glioblastoma that is essentially always sporadic with no established cause; prior therapeutic cranial ionizing radiation is a recognized risk factor for high-grade gliomas generally, and rare cases arise after radiotherapy.
Hemangioblastoma  [VHL]  (aka Central nervous system hemangioblastoma)
    Cause: About one quarter arise in von Hippel-Lindau disease from a germline VHL tumor suppressor mutation, which is the only established genetic cause; the remaining sporadic tumors have no established cause.
High-grade astrocytoma with piloid features  (aka HGAP)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic tumor with no established cause; recurrent MAPK pathway alterations, CDKN2A/B loss, and ATRX changes are somatic events, although rare cases arise on a background of neurofibromatosis type 1.
Infant-type hemispheric glioma  [NTRK1]  (aka IHG)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic infant glioma with no established cause; the recurrent receptor tyrosine kinase fusions (ALK, ROS1, NTRK, MET) are somatic drivers rather than inherited or environmental causes.
Myxoid glioneuronal tumor  [PDGFRA]  (aka MGNT)
    Cause (not established): Rare sporadic tumor with no established cause; the defining PDGFRA dinucleotide mutation is a somatic event rather than an inherited or environmental cause.
Myxopapillary ependymoma  (aka MPE)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic tumor of the filum terminale/conus with no established cause and no recognized hereditary or environmental risk factor.
Oligodendroglioma, isocitrate dehydrogenase-mutant and 1p/19q-codeleted  [IDH1]  (aka Oligodendroglioma, IDH-mutant and 1p/19q-codeleted, Anaplastic oligodendroglioma)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic with no established cause; IDH mutation and 1p/19q codeletion are somatic defining events, not inherited causes. Prior therapeutic ionizing radiation is a recognized risk factor for gliomas in general.
Papillary craniopharyngioma  [BRAF]  (aka PCP)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic tumor of adults with no established cause; the defining somatic BRAF V600E mutation is a driver rather than an inherited or environmental cause.
Papillary glioneuronal tumor  [PRKCA]  (aka PGNT)
    Cause (not established): Rare sporadic glioneuronal tumor with no established cause; the defining PRKCA fusion (typically SLC44A1-PRKCA) is a somatic driver, not a heritable or environmental cause.
Papillary tumor of the pineal region  (aka PTPR)
    Cause (not established): Rare sporadic tumor of the pineal region with no established cause and no recognized hereditary or environmental risk factor.
Pilocytic astrocytoma  [BRAF]  (aka Juvenile pilocytic astrocytoma, Pilomyxoid astrocytoma)
    Cause: Most are sporadic with no known cause, but neurofibromatosis type 1 (germline NF1 mutation) strongly predisposes to pilocytic astrocytoma, particularly optic pathway glioma, in which roughly 15 to 20 percent of children with NF1 develop an optic pathway tumor.
Pineal parenchymal tumor of intermediate differentiation  (aka PPTID)
    Cause (not established): Rare sporadic pineal tumor with no established cause and no recognized hereditary or environmental risk factor.
Pleomorphic xanthoastrocytoma  [BRAF]  (aka PXA, Anaplastic pleomorphic xanthoastrocytoma)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic tumor with no established cause; the frequent BRAF V600E mutation and CDKN2A/B loss are somatic events, not heritable or environmental causes.
Posterior fossa ependymoma, group PFA  (aka PFA ependymoma, Posterior fossa group A ependymoma)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic infant and childhood ependymoma with no established cause; it is defined by a CpG island hypermethylation and EZHIP overexpression epigenetic phenotype (a somatic feature), not by an inherited or environmental cause.
Posterior fossa ependymoma, group PFB  (aka PFB ependymoma, Posterior fossa group B ependymoma)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic posterior fossa ependymoma of older children and adults with no established cause; it is defined by its methylation and cytogenetic profile rather than a heritable or environmental cause.
Primary diffuse large B-cell lymphoma of the central nervous system  [MYD88]  (aka Primary CNS lymphoma, PCNSL, Primary diffuse large B-cell lymphoma of the CNS)
    Cause: In immunodeficient patients (HIV/AIDS, transplant recipients, congenital immunodeficiency) it is caused by Epstein-Barr virus driven B-cell proliferation in the setting of immunosuppression; in immunocompetent patients it is largely sporadic with no established cause.
Rosette-forming glioneuronal tumor  [FGFR1]  (aka RGNT)
    Cause (not established): Rare sporadic tumor, typically of the fourth ventricle, with no established cause; recurrent FGFR1 and PIK3CA/NF1 alterations are somatic events rather than inherited or environmental causes.
Solitary fibrous tumor of the central nervous system  [STAT6]  (aka Hemangiopericytoma, CNS solitary fibrous tumor)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic mesenchymal tumor with no established cause; the defining NAB2-STAT6 fusion is a somatic driver rather than an inherited or environmental cause.
Spinal ependymoma, MYCN-amplified  [MYCN]  (aka SP-EPN-MYCN)
    Cause (not established): Rare aggressive spinal ependymoma with no established cause; the defining MYCN amplification is a somatic driver, not an inherited or environmental cause.
Subependymal giant cell astrocytoma  [TSC1]  (aka SEGA)
    Cause: Occurs almost exclusively in tuberous sclerosis complex, an autosomal dominant disorder from germline TSC1 or TSC2 mutation, with biallelic TSC inactivation driving the tumor; it is therefore a syndrome-defining lesion rather than a sporadic tumor.
Subependymoma
    Cause (not established): Benign, often incidental sporadic tumor with no established cause and no recognized hereditary or environmental risk factor.
Supratentorial ependymoma, YAP1 fusion-positive  [YAP1]  (aka ST-EPN YAP1 fusion-positive)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic ependymoma of young children with no established cause; the defining YAP1 fusion is a somatic driver, not a heritable or environmental cause.
Supratentorial ependymoma, ZFTA fusion-positive  [ZFTA]  (aka ST-EPN ZFTA fusion-positive, RELA fusion-positive ependymoma)
    Cause (not established): Sporadic ependymoma with no established cause; the defining ZFTA (formerly C11orf95) fusion, most often with RELA, is a somatic driver rather than an inherited or environmental cause.

## Hematologic (74)

Acute promyelocytic leukemia with PML-RARA fusion  [PML::RARA]  (aka APL, acute promyelocytic leukaemia)
    Cause (not established): Most cases are sporadic with no established cause; the PML::RARA fusion is a somatic event. A recognized subset is therapy-related, caused by prior treatment with topoisomerase II inhibitors (anthracyclines, mitoxantrone, etoposide) or alkylating agents.
Adult T-cell leukemia/lymphoma  [Human T-cell lymphotropic virus type 1]  (aka ATLL, adult T-cell leukaemia/lymphoma)
    Cause: Caused by chronic infection with human T-cell lymphotropic virus type 1 (HTLV-1), typically acquired in infancy through breastfeeding; HTLV-1 is regarded as the sole cause, with disease arising decades later in about 5 percent of carriers. It is endemic in Japan, the Caribbean, sub-Saharan Africa, and parts of South America.
Aggressive NK-cell leukemia  [Epstein-Barr virus]  (aka ANKL)
    Cause: Strongly associated with Epstein-Barr virus, which is detected in nearly all cases, particularly in East Asian populations; EBV is regarded as central to its pathogenesis.
Aggressive systemic mastocytosis  [KIT D816V]  (aka ASM)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is driven by an acquired KIT D816V mutation, usually with additional myeloid mutations (SRSF2, ASXL1, RUNX1). These are somatic events, not external causes.
ALK-positive histiocytosis  [KIF5B::ALK]  (aka ALK-positive histiocytosis)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not an external exposure; it is defined by acquired ALK rearrangements (usually KIF5B::ALK), a somatic driver. No infectious, environmental, or hereditary cause is established.
Anaplastic large cell lymphoma, ALK-negative  [DUSP22]  (aka ALK-negative ALCL, ALK- anaplastic large cell lymphoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is a systemic T-cell lymphoma defined by acquired lesions (e.g., DUSP22 or TP63 rearrangements) with no confirmed external cause. It is distinct from the breast implant-associated form.
Anaplastic large cell lymphoma, ALK-positive  [ALK]  (aka ALK-positive ALCL, ALK+ anaplastic large cell lymphoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is defined by an acquired ALK rearrangement (usually NPM1::ALK), a somatic driver rather than an external cause. No confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Atypical chronic myeloid leukemia  [SETBP1]  (aka aCML, MDS/MPN with neutrophilia)
    Cause (not established): Cause is generally not established; it is a BCR::ABL1-negative myelodysplastic/myeloproliferative neoplasm driven by acquired mutations (SETBP1, ETNK1, ASXL1). A subset may relate to prior cytotoxic therapy or benzene exposure, but most cases are idiopathic.
B-prolymphocytic leukemia  (aka B-PLL, splenic B-cell lymphoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this rare B-cell leukemia (now largely reclassified within other entities) is idiopathic with no confirmed infectious, environmental, or hereditary cause.
Blastic plasmacytoid dendritic cell neoplasm  [CD123]  (aka BPDCN)
    Cause (not established): No established environmental, infectious, or hereditary cause; occurs mainly in older adults and is defined by recurrent deletions (RB1, CDKN2A, TP53). A minority arise after immunosuppression or alongside other myeloid neoplasms.
Bone marrow mastocytosis  [KIT D816V]  (aka BMM)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is a clonal mast cell disorder driven by an acquired KIT D816V mutation. This somatic driver is not an external cause, and no environmental etiology is confirmed.
Breast implant-associated anaplastic large cell lymphoma  [JAK1]  (aka BIA-ALCL)
    Cause: Caused by textured-surface breast implants placed for reconstruction or cosmesis; an epidemiologic causal relationship is established and the leading mechanism is chronic inflammation from bacterial biofilm on the textured shell in predisposed individuals. Smooth implants are not implicated.
Burkitt lymphoma  [MYC]  (aka BL)
    Cause: The endemic (African) form is caused by Epstein-Barr virus with holoendemic Plasmodium falciparum malaria as a cofactor; the immunodeficiency-associated form is linked to HIV infection. The sporadic form has no established cause and is EBV-positive in only a minority.
Chronic eosinophilic leukemia  [FIP1L1-PDGFRA]  (aka CEL, chronic eosinophilic leukaemia, not otherwise specified)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is a clonal myeloid proliferation defined by acquired genetic lesions, distinct from the tyrosine kinase fusion driven myeloid neoplasms. No external etiologic factor is proven.
Chronic myeloid leukemia in blast phase  [BCR::ABL1]  (aka CML blast phase, CML-BP)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it represents progression of chronic myeloid leukemia, which is defined by the acquired BCR::ABL1 fusion (Philadelphia chromosome). High-dose ionizing radiation is the only proven environmental risk factor for the underlying CML; the fusion itself is a somatic event.
Chronic myelomonocytic leukemia  [TET2]  (aka CMML)
    Cause (not established): Most cases are sporadic with no identified cause; established myeloid leukemia risk factors (benzene exposure, cigarette smoking, prior cytotoxic chemotherapy or radiation) contribute to a subset, and it is a clonal disorder of older adults driven by acquired mutations such as TET2, SRSF2, and ASXL1.
Chronic neutrophilic leukemia  [CSF3R T618I]  (aka CNL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is a rare myeloproliferative neoplasm driven in most cases by acquired activating CSF3R mutations (T618I), a somatic event rather than an external cause.
Early T-cell precursor lymphoblastic leukemia/lymphoma  (aka ETP-ALL, early T-cell precursor ALL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this immature T-lineage subtype is defined by a distinctive immunophenotype and acquired mutations (FLT3, RAS pathway, DNMT3A). No confirmed external cause.
Enteropathy-associated T-cell lymphoma  [JAK1]  (aka EATL)
    Cause: Caused by celiac disease, arising from aberrant intraepithelial T lymphocytes in patients with (often refractory) gluten-sensitive enteropathy. Poor adherence to a gluten-free diet, HLA-DQ2 homozygosity, and late celiac diagnosis are recognized risk factors.
Erdheim-Chester disease  [BRAF]  (aka ECD)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not an external exposure; it is a clonal histiocytic neoplasm driven by somatic MAPK/ERK pathway mutations, most often BRAF V600E. No infectious, environmental, or hereditary cause is established.
Essential thrombocythemia  [JAK2]  (aka ET, essential thrombocythaemia)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is a clonal myeloproliferative neoplasm driven by acquired JAK2, CALR, or MPL mutations. These are somatic events, not external causes.
Extranodal NK/T-cell lymphoma, nasal type  [Epstein-Barr virus]  (aka ENKTL, extranodal NK/T-cell lymphoma)
    Cause: Caused by Epstein-Barr virus infection of the neoplastic NK or T cells in genetically susceptible individuals; EBV is present in essentially all cases and is central to pathogenesis. It is most common in East Asian and Latin American populations.
Extraosseous plasmacytoma  (aka extramedullary plasmacytoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this localized extramedullary clonal plasma cell tumor, most often in the upper aerodigestive tract, is idiopathic with no confirmed external cause.
Follicular dendritic cell sarcoma  (aka FDCS)
    Cause: Cause is generally not established; most are sporadic. A distinct subset, the inflammatory pseudotumor-like variant arising in liver and spleen, is caused by Epstein-Barr virus.
Hairy cell leukemia  [BRAF]  (aka HCL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; the near-universal BRAF V600E mutation is a somatic driver, not an external etiology. Some studies report weak associations with prior farming, pesticide, or radiation exposure, but no risk factor is confirmed.
Hepatosplenic T cell lymphoma  [STAT5B]  (aka HSTL)
    Cause: Most cases arise de novo without an identified cause, but roughly 20 percent occur in the setting of chronic immunosuppression or immune dysregulation, notably long-term thiopurine and anti-TNF therapy for inflammatory bowel disease and prior solid organ transplantation.
Histiocytic sarcoma  [MAP2K1]  (aka HS)
    Cause (not established): Cause is generally not established; it is a rare malignant histiocytic neoplasm often driven by MAPK pathway mutations. A subset is transdifferentiated (clonally related) from a preceding B-cell lymphoma/leukemia or from germ cell tumors, but no external cause is confirmed.
Idiopathic multicentric Castleman disease  [interleukin 6]  (aka iMCD, multicentric Castleman disease)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established (hence idiopathic); it is an HHV-8-negative systemic inflammatory disorder driven by an unexplained cytokine storm, typically interleukin-6. The source of the hypercytokinemia and any triggering factor remain unknown.
Immunoglobulin light chain amyloidosis  (aka AL amyloidosis, primary amyloidosis)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it results from a clonal plasma cell (or, less often, B-cell) proliferation producing misfolded free light chains that deposit as amyloid. The underlying clone is driven by acquired lesions; no external cause is confirmed.
Indolent systemic mastocytosis  [KIT D816V]  (aka ISM)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; about 95 percent are driven by an acquired KIT D816V gain-of-function mutation, a somatic event, not an external exposure. Rare familial forms exist but the vast majority are sporadic.
Indolent T-cell lymphoma of the gastrointestinal tract  [JAK2]  (aka indolent clonal T-cell lymphoproliferative disorder of the gastrointestinal tract)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this indolent clonal T-cell lymphoproliferative disorder of the gut is idiopathic, sometimes with chronic mucosal inflammation, and is driven by acquired lesions (e.g., STAT3::JAK2 fusion, JAK/STAT pathway mutations). No confirmed external cause.
Interdigitating dendritic cell sarcoma  (aka IDCS)
    Cause (not established): Cause is generally not established; this rare dendritic cell neoplasm is idiopathic. A minority are clonally related to a preceding low-grade B-cell lymphoma (transdifferentiation), but no external cause is confirmed.
Intravascular large B-cell lymphoma  [MYD88 L265P]  (aka IVLBCL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this rare large B-cell lymphoma growing within blood vessels is idiopathic, driven by acquired lesions (frequent MYD88 and CD79B mutations). No confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Juvenile myelomonocytic leukemia  [PTPN11]  (aka JMML)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not an external exposure; it is driven by acquired or, in a subset, germline RAS pathway mutations. Children with neurofibromatosis type 1 and with Noonan syndrome (germline PTPN11) are predisposed, but there is no confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Juvenile xanthogranuloma  [MAP2K1]  (aka JXG)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this histiocytic proliferation of infants and children is usually benign and idiopathic, with a subset harboring MAPK pathway mutations. An association with neurofibromatosis type 1 and juvenile myelomonocytic leukemia is recognized in a minority.
KSHV/HHV8-associated multicentric Castleman disease  [Human herpesvirus 8]  (aka HHV8-associated multicentric Castleman disease)
    Cause: Caused by uncontrolled infection with Kaposi sarcoma-associated herpesvirus (KSHV/HHV-8); it is most often diagnosed in people with HIV infection or other immunosuppression, in whom impaired control of HHV-8 drives the lymphoproliferation.
Langerhans cell histiocytosis  [BRAF]  (aka LCH)
    Cause: Now recognized as a clonal neoplasm driven by somatic MAPK pathway mutations (BRAF V600E in about half of cases); these are acquired drivers not external causes. The pulmonary adult form is a distinct entity strongly caused by cigarette (and marijuana) smoking.
Langerhans cell sarcoma  [BRAF V600E]  (aka LCS)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this rare high-grade Langerhans cell neoplasm is driven by MAPK pathway mutations (including BRAF V600E) and may arise de novo or from progression of Langerhans cell histiocytosis. No confirmed external cause.
Mantle cell lymphoma  [CCND1]
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; the defining t(11;14) CCND1 rearrangement is a somatic event. No confirmed infectious, environmental, or hereditary cause, though it is a clonal B-cell malignancy of older adults.
Mast cell leukemia  [KIT D816V]  (aka MCL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; like other mastocytoses it is driven by an acquired activating KIT mutation (usually D816V), a somatic event rather than an external cause. No environmental or infectious cause is proven.
Mast cell sarcoma  [KIT]  (aka MCS)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this exceedingly rare localized malignant mast cell tumor is idiopathic, with variable KIT mutation status and no confirmed external cause.
Mixed phenotype acute leukemia  [BCR::ABL1]  (aka MPAL, acute leukemia of ambiguous lineage)
    Cause (not established): Most cases are sporadic with no established cause; some are therapy-related after prior cytotoxic chemotherapy or radiation. It is defined by acquired genetic lesions and lineage-ambiguous immunophenotype.
Monomorphic epitheliotropic intestinal T-cell lymphoma  [SETD2]  (aka MEITL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; unlike EATL it is NOT associated with celiac disease. It arises from intestinal intraepithelial T lymphocytes, is more common in Asian and Hispanic populations, and is driven by acquired mutations (e.g., SETD2, STAT5B).
Mycosis fungoides  (aka Cutaneous T cell lymphoma, MF)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this cutaneous T-cell lymphoma is largely idiopathic. Proposed but unproven contributors include chronic antigen stimulation and environmental or occupational exposures; no infectious agent is confirmed.
Myeloid/lymphoid neoplasm with eosinophilia and ETV6::ABL1 fusion  [ETV6::ABL1]  (aka MLN-eo with ETV6::ABL1 fusion)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is defined by an acquired ETV6::ABL1 fusion, a somatic tyrosine kinase fusion, not an external cause.
Myeloid/lymphoid neoplasm with eosinophilia and FGFR1 rearrangement  [FGFR1]  (aka MLN-eo with FGFR1 rearrangement, 8p11 myeloproliferative syndrome)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is defined by an acquired FGFR1 rearrangement, a somatic tyrosine kinase fusion, not an external cause.
Myeloid/lymphoid neoplasm with eosinophilia and FLT3 rearrangement  [FLT3]  (aka MLN-eo with FLT3 rearrangement)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is defined by an acquired FLT3 rearrangement, a somatic tyrosine kinase fusion, not an external cause.
Myeloid/lymphoid neoplasm with eosinophilia and JAK2 rearrangement  [PCM1::JAK2]  (aka MLN-eo with JAK2 rearrangement)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is defined by an acquired JAK2 rearrangement (e.g., PCM1::JAK2), a somatic fusion, not an external cause.
Myeloid/lymphoid neoplasm with eosinophilia and PDGFRA rearrangement  [FIP1L1::PDGFRA]  (aka MLN-eo with PDGFRA rearrangement)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is defined by an acquired PDGFRA rearrangement (usually the FIP1L1::PDGFRA fusion), a somatic driver rather than an external cause.
Myeloid/lymphoid neoplasm with eosinophilia and PDGFRB rearrangement  [ETV6::PDGFRB]  (aka MLN-eo with PDGFRB rearrangement)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is defined by an acquired PDGFRB rearrangement, a somatic tyrosine kinase fusion, not an external cause.
NK-cell large granular lymphocytic leukemia  [STAT3]  (aka NK-LGLL, chronic lymphoproliferative disorder of NK cells)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this chronic NK-cell lymphoproliferation is idiopathic, associated with chronic immune activation and in some series with prior Epstein-Barr virus exposure, but no confirmed causal agent.
Nodal T-follicular helper cell lymphoma, angioimmunoblastic type  [RHOA G17V]  (aka angioimmunoblastic T-cell lymphoma, AITL, nodal TFH cell lymphoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it arises from follicular helper T cells and is driven by acquired mutations (TET2, DNMT3A, IDH2, RHOA G17V). Epstein-Barr virus positive bystander B cells are characteristically present but EBV is not the neoplastic driver.
Plasma cell leukemia  (aka PCL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is an aggressive clonal plasma cell malignancy, arising de novo or evolving from multiple myeloma, driven by acquired cytogenetic abnormalities. No confirmed external cause.
Plasmablastic lymphoma  [MYC]  (aka PBL)
    Cause: Strongly associated with immunodeficiency, especially HIV/AIDS and iatrogenic immunosuppression after solid organ transplant, and Epstein-Barr virus is detected in most cases; MYC rearrangement is a frequent cofactor. The full pathogenesis remains incompletely defined.
Polycythemia vera  [JAK2]  (aka PV, polycythaemia vera)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is a clonal myeloproliferative neoplasm caused by an acquired JAK2 mutation (V617F in most cases), which is a somatic driver rather than an external cause. No confirmed environmental etiology.
Primary cutaneous CD30-positive T-cell lymphoproliferative disorder  [DUSP22]  (aka primary cutaneous anaplastic large cell lymphoma, lymphomatoid papulosis)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this spectrum (lymphomatoid papulosis and primary cutaneous anaplastic large cell lymphoma) is idiopathic. It is defined by CD30 expression and acquired lesions, with no confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Primary cutaneous CD8-positive aggressive epidermotropic cytotoxic T-cell lymphoma  (aka primary cutaneous aggressive epidermotropic CD8-positive cytotoxic T-cell lymphoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this rare aggressive cutaneous cytotoxic T-cell lymphoma is idiopathic with no confirmed external cause.
Primary cutaneous diffuse large B-cell lymphoma, leg type  [MYD88 L265P]  (aka PCDLBCL-LT, primary cutaneous diffuse large B-cell lymphoma leg type)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this aggressive cutaneous B-cell lymphoma of elderly patients is driven by acquired lesions (MYD88 L265P, BCL2/MYC) with no confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Primary cutaneous follicle center lymphoma  (aka PCFCL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is generally not established; it is an indolent cutaneous B-cell lymphoma of follicle center cells. Borrelia burgdorferi has been proposed as a cofactor in some European cases but the association is not confirmed, and most cases are idiopathic.
Primary cutaneous gamma/delta T-cell lymphoma  (aka PCGD-TCL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this aggressive cutaneous lymphoma of gamma/delta T cells is idiopathic with no confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Primary cutaneous marginal zone lymphoma  (aka PCMZL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is generally not established; it is an indolent cutaneous B-cell lymphoma. Chronic antigenic stimulation, including Borrelia burgdorferi infection in some European cohorts, has been implicated as a cofactor but is not confirmed, and most cases are idiopathic.
Primary effusion lymphoma  [Human herpesvirus 8]  (aka PEL)
    Cause: Caused by Kaposi sarcoma-associated herpesvirus (KSHV/HHV-8), which is necessary for the disease; it occurs almost exclusively in the setting of severe immunosuppression, most often advanced HIV/AIDS, and HIV-positive cases are frequently co-infected with Epstein-Barr virus.
Primary mediastinal large B-cell lymphoma  [CD274]  (aka PMBL, primary mediastinal B-cell lymphoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this thymic B-cell lymphoma of young adults is driven by acquired lesions (9p24/JAK2 and PD-L1/PD-L2 amplification, NF-kB pathway alterations). No confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Primary myelofibrosis  [JAK2]  (aka PMF)
    Cause (not established): Cause is generally not established; it is a clonal myeloproliferative neoplasm driven by acquired JAK2 V617F, CALR, or MPL mutations, which are somatic events. Ionizing radiation and benzene exposure are recognized but uncommon contributors.
Rosai-Dorfman disease  [MAP2K1]  (aka RDD, sinus histiocytosis with massive lymphadenopathy)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is regarded as idiopathic, with a subset harboring somatic MAPK pathway (KRAS, MAP2K1) mutations and rare familial forms tied to SLC29A3 mutations. No confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Sezary syndrome
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; the leukemic erythrodermic form of cutaneous T-cell lymphoma is idiopathic with no confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Smoldering systemic mastocytosis  [KIT D816V]  (aka SSM, smouldering systemic mastocytosis)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is a clonal mast cell disorder driven by an acquired KIT D816V mutation, a somatic driver rather than an external cause.
Solitary plasmacytoma of bone  (aka solitary bone plasmacytoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is a localized clonal plasma cell tumor and part of the plasma cell neoplasm spectrum. No confirmed infectious, environmental, or hereditary cause.
Splenic B-cell lymphoma/leukemia with prominent nucleoli  [MAP2K1]  (aka SBLPN, hairy cell leukemia variant, HCL-variant)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; this rare splenic small B-cell neoplasm (formerly splenic diffuse red pulp lymphoma / hairy cell leukemia variant spectrum) is idiopathic with no confirmed infectious or environmental cause.
Subcutaneous panniculitis-like T-cell lymphoma  [HAVCR2]  (aka SPTCL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is idiopathic and frequently associated with autoimmunity. A subset arises in patients with germline HAVCR2 (TIM-3) mutations that predispose to an associated hemophagocytic syndrome, but most cases have no external cause.
Systemic mastocytosis with an associated hematologic neoplasm  [KIT D816V]  (aka SM-AHN)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; the mast cell clone and the accompanying myeloid neoplasm share acquired mutations (KIT D816V plus SRSF2/ASXL1/RUNX1). These are somatic drivers, not external causes.
T-cell large granular lymphocytic leukemia  [STAT3]  (aka T-LGLL, T-cell large granular lymphocytic leukaemia)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established, but it is strongly linked to chronic immune stimulation and autoimmune disease, notably rheumatoid arthritis (overlapping with Felty syndrome), and is driven by acquired activating STAT3 mutations. No external etiologic agent is proven.
T-lymphoblastic leukemia/lymphoma  [NOTCH1]  (aka T-ALL, T-LBL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is generally not established; it is driven by acquired mutations (e.g., NOTCH1) and is a sporadic malignancy. Prior chemotherapy or radiation and inherited cancer predisposition syndromes contribute to a minority, but most cases are idiopathic.
T-prolymphocytic leukemia  [TCL1A]  (aka T-PLL)
    Cause (not established): Cause is not established; it is driven by acquired chromosome 14 inversions/translocations activating TCL1 and by ATM inactivation. Patients with the hereditary ATM-deficiency disorder ataxia-telangiectasia are predisposed, but sporadic cases have no external cause.

## Additional catalogued rare cancers (64)

Acute promyelocytic leukemia  [PML-RARA]  (aka APL)
    Cause: Cause largely not established; defined by the t(15;17) PML::RARA fusion and usually arises de novo, though therapy-related cases occur after prior exposure to topoisomerase II inhibitor chemotherapy.
Adrenal neuroblastoma  [MYCN]
    Cause: Cause largely not established; most are sporadic embryonal tumors of neural crest origin, with a minority hereditary due to germline ALK mutations or PHOX2B mutations (the latter in congenital central hypoventilation and Hirschsprung syndrome).
Anal cancer  [HPV]  (aka Anal squamous cell carcinoma, Squamous cell carcinoma of the anus, Anal canal SCC)
    Cause: Caused predominantly by persistent high-risk human papillomavirus (especially HPV-16) infection; risk is amplified by HIV infection, immunosuppression, receptive anal intercourse, and smoking. Precursor lesion is anal intraepithelial neoplasia.
Anaplastic large cell lymphoma  [ALK]  (aka ALCL)
    Cause: Cause largely not established for systemic ALK-positive and ALK-negative forms; the breast implant-associated variant is a clear exception, causally linked to textured breast implants with chronic inflammation.
Angioimmunoblastic T cell lymphoma  [TET2]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; frequently associated with Epstein-Barr virus in the tumor microenvironment and driven by recurrent T follicular helper cell mutations (TET2, DNMT3A, RHOA, IDH2), but no confirmed etiologic cause. Largely sporadic.
Appendiceal cancer  [KRAS]  (aka Appendix cancer, Appendiceal adenocarcinoma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; most appendiceal tumors, including mucinous neoplasms and appendiceal neuroendocrine tumors, arise sporadically with no confirmed environmental or hereditary etiology.
Carcinoma of unknown primary  (aka CUP)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established by definition; it is metastatic carcinoma without an identifiable primary site, so no single etiology applies, though it reflects underlying carcinomas whose own risk factors (smoking, alcohol, and others) may be relevant.
Cholangiocarcinoma  [FGFR2]  (aka Bile duct cancer, Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma, iCCA, Intrahepatic bile duct carcinoma)
    Cause: Chronic biliary inflammation is the main driver: primary sclerosing cholangitis, liver fluke infection (Opisthorchis viverrini, Clonorchis sinensis), hepatolithiasis, choledochal cysts, chronic hepatitis B or C, cirrhosis, and thorotrast exposure. Precursor lesions include biliary intraepithelial neoplasia and intraductal papillary neoplasm of the bile duct.
Chondrosarcoma  [IDH1]
    Cause: Most conventional cases are sporadic, but a subset arises from precursor cartilage lesions such as enchondromas (Ollier disease, Maffucci syndrome) and osteochondromas (hereditary multiple exostoses due to EXT1/EXT2 mutations).
Chordoma  [Brachyury]
    Cause: Arises from notochordal remnants along the axial skeleton; a benign notochordal cell tumor is a precursor, and germline duplication of the T (brachyury/TBXT) gene predisposes in familial chordoma. Most cases are otherwise sporadic.
Clear cell sarcoma  [EWSR1-ATF1]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this melanocytic soft tissue sarcoma is defined by an EWSR1::ATF1 fusion but has no known inherited or environmental cause, and is sporadic.
Collecting duct carcinoma of the kidney
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this aggressive renal cancer arises from the distal collecting duct with no confirmed inherited or environmental etiology, and is generally sporadic.
Craniopharyngioma  [CTNNB1]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; adamantinomatous type arises from embryonic Rathke pouch epithelial remnants with CTNNB1 mutations, and papillary type carries BRAF V600E mutations, but neither has a known inherited or environmental cause. Sporadic.
Cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma
    Cause: Chronic cumulative ultraviolet (sun) exposure is the dominant cause; other factors include immunosuppression (organ transplant, HIV), human papillomavirus, chronic wounds and scars (Marjolin ulcer), arsenic and radiation exposure, and genodermatoses such as xeroderma pigmentosum and epidermolysis bullosa.
Desmoid tumor  [CTNNB1]  (aka Aggressive fibromatosis)
    Cause: Predisposed by germline APC mutations in familial adenomatous polyposis (Gardner syndrome); sporadic desmoids are driven by CTNNB1 (beta-catenin) mutations and are associated with prior trauma or surgery, pregnancy, and estrogen exposure.
Diffuse intrinsic pontine glioma  [H3F3A]  (aka DIPG, Diffuse midline glioma)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this pediatric brainstem glioma is defined by histone H3 K27 mutations and has no known inherited or environmental cause. Sporadic.
Endometrial stromal sarcoma  [JAZF1]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; defined by recurrent gene fusions (for example JAZF1::SUZ12) but has no confirmed inherited or environmental cause, and tamoxifen use and unopposed estrogen have been weakly implicated. Generally sporadic.
Ependymoma  [RELA]
    Cause: Mostly sporadic; the main recognized hereditary predisposition is neurofibromatosis type 2 (germline NF2), which is associated with spinal ependymomas. No established environmental cause.
Extragonadal germ cell tumor
    Cause: Predisposing conditions include Klinefelter syndrome (associated with mediastinal germ cell tumors) and gonadal dysgenesis; these arise from misplaced primordial germ cells along the midline and are otherwise largely of unestablished cause.
Extranodal NK/T cell lymphoma  [Epstein-Barr virus]
    Cause: Causally associated with Epstein-Barr virus infection, which is present in essentially all cases; it is most common in East Asian and Latin American populations.
Fibrosarcoma
    Cause (not established): Cause largely not established; adult fibrosarcoma is usually sporadic, and infantile fibrosarcoma is defined by an ETV6::NTRK3 fusion. Prior radiation is a recognized cause of some secondary fibrosarcomas.
Gallbladder cancer  [TP53]
    Cause: Strongly linked to chronic inflammation from gallstones (cholelithiasis), porcelain (calcified) gallbladder, chronic Salmonella typhi (typhoid) carriage, and gallbladder polyps; anomalous pancreaticobiliary duct junction and obesity are additional risk factors.
Gastrointestinal neuroendocrine tumor  (aka Carcinoid tumor)
    Cause: Most are sporadic with no established cause, but recognized predisposing conditions include multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN1), and for gastric types, chronic atrophic gastritis with hypergastrinemia (autoimmune or Zollinger-Ellison syndrome).
Gestational trophoblastic disease  (aka Choriocarcinoma, Hydatidiform mole)
    Cause: Arises from abnormal fertilization producing hydatidiform mole (complete moles from androgenetic diploidy, partial moles from triploid dispermic fertilization); a prior molar pregnancy and extremes of maternal age are risk factors, and rare familial recurrent hydatidiform mole is caused by NLRP7 or KHDC3L mutations.
Glioblastoma  [EGFR]  (aka GBM)
    Cause: Cause not established for the vast majority; the only firmly established environmental risk factor is prior ionizing radiation to the head, and rare cases occur in hereditary syndromes (Li-Fraumeni, Lynch/constitutional mismatch repair deficiency, neurofibromatosis). Otherwise sporadic.
Granulosa cell tumor of the ovary  [FOXL2]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; adult type is characterized by a somatic FOXL2 mutation but has no known inherited or environmental cause, and juvenile type can occur in enchondromatosis syndromes (Ollier disease, Maffucci syndrome). Generally sporadic.
Hurthle cell thyroid carcinoma  (aka Oncocytic thyroid carcinoma, Hurthle cell carcinoma, oxyphilic follicular carcinoma)
    Cause: Ionizing radiation exposure to the head and neck, especially in childhood, is an established risk factor; chronic autoimmune thyroiditis (Hashimoto) and iodine deficiency are associated, and rare familial nonmedullary thyroid cancer clustering occurs, but most cases are sporadic.
Inflammatory breast cancer  (aka IBC)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; it is an aggressive clinical presentation of breast carcinoma without a specific etiologic factor, though obesity and general breast cancer risk factors apply and it is usually sporadic.
Large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma  (aka LCNEC)
    Cause: When pulmonary, it is strongly caused by tobacco smoking, similar to small cell lung cancer; large cell neuroendocrine carcinomas at other sites are largely of unestablished cause.
Liposarcoma  [MDM2]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; liposarcomas are generally sporadic soft tissue tumors defined by characteristic genetic changes (for example MDM2 amplification) without a confirmed inherited or environmental etiology.
Low grade serous ovarian carcinoma  [KRAS]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; arises from serous borderline (atypical proliferative) tumor as a precursor lesion but has no confirmed inherited or environmental etiologic cause, and is generally sporadic.
Male breast cancer  [BRCA2]
    Cause: Predisposed by germline BRCA2 (and BRCA1) mutations, Klinefelter syndrome, conditions of estrogen excess or androgen deficiency (obesity, cirrhosis, testicular disorders), prior chest radiation, and family history.
Malignant mesothelioma  [BAP1]  (aka Pleural mesothelioma, malignant pleural mesothelioma, diffuse pleural mesothelioma, epithelioid mesothelioma, sarcomatoid mesothelioma, biphasic mesothelioma)
    Cause: Asbestos exposure is the established cause in the great majority of cases, with a long latency of decades; erionite fiber exposure and prior chest radiation are additional causes, and germline BAP1 mutation predisposes.
Medulloblastoma  [MYC]
    Cause: Mostly sporadic, but predisposed by hereditary syndromes: Gorlin (nevoid basal cell carcinoma) syndrome and other SHH-pathway germline defects for SHH-activated tumors, and germline APC (Turcot syndrome) for WNT-activated tumors, as well as Li-Fraumeni syndrome.
Meningioma  [NF2]
    Cause: Prior ionizing radiation to the head is an established cause; neurofibromatosis type 2 (germline NF2) predisposes to multiple meningiomas, and hormonal factors are associated. Many cases are otherwise sporadic.
Multicentric Castleman disease  [HHV-8]
    Cause: A large fraction is caused by uncontrolled human herpesvirus-8 (HHV-8) infection, typically in the setting of HIV or immunosuppression; the remaining HHV-8-negative idiopathic form has an unknown cause driven by IL-6-mediated cytokine storm.
Nonseminomatous germ cell tumor
    Cause: Shares testicular germ cell tumor risk factors, notably cryptorchidism, family or personal history, and testicular dysgenesis; develops from germ cell neoplasia in situ.
Oligodendroglioma  [IDH1]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; defined by IDH mutation and 1p/19q codeletion but with no confirmed inherited or environmental cause. Prior brain irradiation is a general risk factor for gliomas; otherwise sporadic.
Osteosarcoma  [TP53]  (aka Osteogenic sarcoma)
    Cause: Predisposed by hereditary cancer syndromes (hereditary retinoblastoma with germline RB1, Li-Fraumeni syndrome with germline TP53, Rothmund-Thomson, Werner, Bloom syndromes), prior therapeutic radiation, and Paget disease of bone in older adults.
Ovarian germ cell tumor
    Cause (not established): Cause largely not established; these tumors arise from primordial germ cells and are usually sporadic, with gonadal dysgenesis (dysgenetic gonads, especially with Y chromosome material) being a recognized predisposing condition for some subtypes.
Papillary renal cell carcinoma  [MET]
    Cause: Hereditary forms have defined causes: hereditary papillary RCC from germline MET mutations (type 1) and hereditary leiomyomatosis and RCC from germline fumarate hydratase mutations (type 2); acquired cystic kidney disease and end-stage renal disease are acquired risk factors, but most cases are sporadic.
PEComa  [TFE3]  (aka Perivascular epithelioid cell tumor)
    Cause: A subset arises in the tuberous sclerosis complex (germline TSC1/TSC2 mutations); most sporadic PEComas have no established inherited or environmental cause.
Penile cancer  [HPV]
    Cause: Caused by high-risk HPV infection in a large fraction of cases; other risk factors include phimosis with chronic inflammation, lack of neonatal circumcision, lichen sclerosus, smoking, and poor hygiene. Precursor is penile intraepithelial neoplasia.
Primary central nervous system lymphoma  [MYD88]  (aka PCNSL)
    Cause: Severe immunosuppression is the main established cause, especially HIV/AIDS (with Epstein-Barr virus driving the tumor) and iatrogenic immunosuppression after transplantation; immunocompetent cases are otherwise of unestablished cause.
Pulmonary carcinoid tumor  (aka Bronchial carcinoid)
    Cause: Cause largely not established; most pulmonary carcinoids are not linked to smoking, and a minority occur in the setting of multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (germline MEN1 mutation) or arise from diffuse idiopathic pulmonary neuroendocrine cell hyperplasia as a precursor.
Rhabdomyosarcoma  [PAX3-FOXO1]
    Cause: Mostly sporadic, but predisposed by hereditary syndromes including Li-Fraumeni syndrome (germline TP53), neurofibromatosis type 1, DICER1 syndrome, Costello syndrome, and Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome.
Sacrococcygeal teratoma
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; this congenital germ cell tumor arises from pluripotent cells near the coccyx (Hensen node remnants) and is generally sporadic, with rare familial presacral teratoma linked to Currarino syndrome (MNX1 mutation).
Salivary gland carcinoma
    Cause: Prior ionizing radiation to the head and neck is the best-established risk factor; some subtypes are associated with defined genetic fusions rather than external causes, and most cases are otherwise sporadic.
Sarcomatoid carcinoma
    Cause (not established): Cause not established as a distinct entity; it represents dedifferentiation of an underlying epithelial carcinoma (for example of lung, kidney, or urothelium), so its risk factors are those of the parent carcinoma rather than a specific separate cause.
Seminoma  [KIT]
    Cause: Same predisposing factors as other testicular germ cell tumors, chiefly cryptorchidism, prior testicular tumor, family history, and testicular dysgenesis; arises from germ cell neoplasia in situ.
Small bowel adenocarcinoma  (aka Small intestine cancer)
    Cause: Predisposed by inherited syndromes (Lynch syndrome, familial adenomatous polyposis, Peutz-Jeghers syndrome) and by chronic small bowel inflammation, notably Crohn disease and celiac disease.
Small cell lung cancer  [RB1]  (aka SCLC)
    Cause: Overwhelmingly caused by tobacco smoking; radon exposure and occupational carcinogens are additional causes. It very rarely occurs in never-smokers.
Solitary fibrous tumor  [NAB2-STAT6]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; defined by a NAB2::STAT6 fusion but without a confirmed inherited or environmental etiologic factor. Generally sporadic.
Spindle cell sarcoma
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; spindle cell sarcoma is a morphologic category encompassing several entities with no single confirmed etiology, and most are sporadic (some secondary to prior radiation).
Systemic mastocytosis  [KIT]
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; the great majority carry an acquired somatic KIT D816V mutation but there is no confirmed inherited or environmental cause, and rare familial cases with germline KIT variants exist. Largely sporadic.
T cell prolymphocytic leukemia  [TCL1A]  (aka T-PLL)
    Cause: Cause largely not established; strongly associated with inversion/translocation of the TCL1 locus, and markedly increased in ataxia-telangiectasia (germline ATM mutation), but sporadic cases have no known external cause.
Testicular germ cell tumor  [KIT]  (aka Testicular cancer)
    Cause: Predisposed by cryptorchidism (undescended testis), personal or family history of testicular cancer, testicular dysgenesis, infertility, and Klinefelter syndrome; germ cell neoplasia in situ is the precursor lesion.
Translocation renal cell carcinoma  [TFE3]  (aka MiT family translocation RCC)
    Cause: Prior exposure to cytotoxic chemotherapy in childhood is the only established risk factor; the tumors are driven by MiT family (TFE3/TFEB) gene fusions, but most arise sporadically without a known external cause.
Urachal carcinoma
    Cause: Arises from persistent embryonic urachal remnants at the bladder dome; no definitive environmental or hereditary risk factors are established, and it is otherwise considered sporadic.
Urethral cancer
    Cause: Associated with high-risk HPV infection (especially distal urethral squamous cancers), chronic urethral inflammation, urethral strictures, and recurrent urinary tract infections; a history of bladder cancer also raises risk.
Vaginal cancer  [HPV]
    Cause: Most squamous vaginal cancers are caused by persistent high-risk human papillomavirus infection; clear cell adenocarcinoma of the vagina is linked to in utero diethylstilbestrol (DES) exposure. Precursor is vaginal intraepithelial neoplasia.
Vulvar cancer  [HPV]  (aka Vulvar squamous cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma of the vulva)
    Cause: Two pathways: high-risk HPV infection (with usual-type vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia) and HPV-independent disease arising in chronic inflammatory dermatoses such as lichen sclerosus with differentiated vulvar intraepithelial neoplasia; smoking and immunosuppression add risk.
Waldenstrom macroglobulinemia  [MYD88]  (aka Lymphoplasmacytic lymphoma, LPL, Waldenstrom macroglobulinaemia)
    Cause (not established): Cause not established; nearly all cases carry a somatic MYD88 L265P mutation and it is preceded by IgM monoclonal gammopathy of undetermined significance, with familial clustering suggesting inherited predisposition, but no confirmed environmental cause. Largely sporadic.
Yolk sac tumor  [AFP]  (aka Endodermal sinus tumor)
    Cause (not established): Cause largely not established; this germ cell tumor arises from primordial germ cells and is usually sporadic, with gonadal dysgenesis being a predisposing condition for gonadal cases.

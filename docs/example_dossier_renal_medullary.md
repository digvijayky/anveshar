# Renal Medullary Carcinoma (RMC): Evidence Dossier

**Purpose.** Clinical decision-support evidence base for renal medullary carcinoma, with an emphasis on cross-cancer translatable therapies and tissue-agnostic precedents. RMC is an ultra-rare, highly aggressive kidney cancer defined by complete loss of SMARCB1 (INI1/BAF47), placing it in the SWI/SNF-deficient rhabdoid tumor family alongside epithelioid sarcoma, atypical teratoid/rhabdoid tumor (AT/RT), and malignant rhabdoid tumor (MRT). Every non-trivial claim is tied to a real PubMed PMID, DOI, or ClinicalTrials.gov NCT number. Items that could not be verified are marked **UNVERIFIED**.

Sources: PubMed (attribution required; DOIs linked inline), ClinicalTrials.gov API v2, FDA/NCI approval announcements, Consensus, and Paperclip. Compiled July 2026.

## 1. Disease Overview

**What it is / cell of origin.** RMC is a rare, undifferentiated, highly lethal epithelial malignancy that arises in the renal medulla (from the collecting-duct/calyceal epithelium) and is almost exclusively associated with sickle cell trait or other hemoglobinopathy. It was originally described as "the seventh sickle cell nephropathy." It is defined molecularly by complete, biallelic loss of the SMARCB1 (INI1/BAF47) tumor suppressor, a core subunit of the SWI/SNF (BAF) chromatin-remodeling complex (Agaimy & Hartmann, *Pathologe* 2021, PMID 34609565, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00292-021-00985-y); Beckermann et al., *J Oncol Pract* 2017, PMID 28697319, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1200/JOP.2017.020909)).

**Epidemiology / demographics.** RMC predominantly afflicts young individuals of African descent who carry sickle cell trait. In a pooled analysis of all reported cases since 1995 (166 patients), median age was 21 years and 98% of cases had sickle cell trait (Iacovelli et al., *Can Urol Assoc J* 2015, PMID 26085875, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5489/cuaj.2373)). In a single-institution series of 36 patients, median age was 28 years (range 12 to 72) and all tested patients had sickle cell trait (Carlo et al., *Clin Genitourin Cancer* 2017, PMID 28558987, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clgc.2017.04.012)). RMC comprises roughly 1% of all renal cell carcinoma diagnoses, with a male predominance (male:female ~2:1) and a median age near 22 (Wei et al., *Genes Chromosomes Cancer* 2020, PMID 32259323, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/gcc.22847)). A cytology series of 12 patients confirmed the demographic (age 13 to 37, median 21.5; all of Black/African descent; 10/12 with confirmed sickle cell trait) (Miller et al., *J Am Soc Cytopathol* 2020, PMID 32651128, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jasc.2020.05.016)).

**Presentation and prognosis.** Most patients present with metastatic disease (lung, lymph nodes, bone), and outcomes are among the worst of all renal cancers. In the pooled literature analysis, overall survival at diagnosis was 4.0 months for metastatic patients and 17.0 months for non-metastatic patients, and advanced stage independently tripled the risk of death (Iacovelli et al. 2015, PMID 26085875, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5489/cuaj.2373)). In the MD Anderson molecular series, median overall survival was 13 months (Wei et al. 2020, PMID 32259323, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/gcc.22847)). In the MSKCC series, overall survival was 5.8 months and, for the 12 patients who received platinum-based therapy, median progression-free survival was only 2.5 months (Carlo et al. 2017, PMID 28558987, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clgc.2017.04.012)). CNS spread almost never occurs and is a notable rarity (Gubbiotti et al., *Acta Neuropathol Commun* 2025, PMID 39833894, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1186/s40478-025-01929-w)).

**Diagnostic definition.** Diagnosis rests on the triad of (1) a poorly differentiated renal medullary carcinoma in a young patient, (2) sickle cell hemoglobinopathy (usually trait), and (3) complete loss of SMARCB1/INI1 nuclear expression by immunohistochemistry (Beckermann et al. 2017, PMID 28697319, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1200/JOP.2017.020909); Trpkov et al., GUPS update, *Mod Pathol* 2021, PMID 33664427, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41379-021-00779-w)). RMC belongs to the broader SMARCB1-deficient tumor family together with MRT, AT/RT, and epithelioid sarcoma (Pawel, *Pediatr Dev Pathol* 2017, PMID 29280680, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1177/1093526617749671); Agaimy, *Adv Anat Pathol* 2014, PMID 25299309, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1097/PAP.0000000000000038)).

## 2. Molecular Drivers

**SMARCB1 (INI1/BAF47) loss is the defining and essentially sole recurrent driver.** RMC is characterized by uniform, complete loss of SMARCB1, a core SWI/SNF subunit. In the MSKCC series, FISH revealed loss of heterozygosity with concurrent translocation in 8 of 10 tumors and biallelic loss in 2; crucially, next-generation targeted sequencing showed no other recurring mutations, underscoring that SMARCB1 loss is the central molecular event and that RMC has an otherwise remarkably quiet coding genome (Carlo et al. 2017, PMID 28558987, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clgc.2017.04.012)). Loss occurs mostly through translocations and deletions rather than point mutation.

**Replication stress and MYC (targetable vulnerability).** Comprehensive genomic and transcriptomic profiling of untreated primary RMC tissues found that RMC is characterized by high replication stress and an abundance of focal copy-number alterations, driven substantially by MYC. This replication stress confers a therapeutic vulnerability to drugs targeting DNA-damage repair pathways (Msaouel et al., *Cancer Cell* 2020, PMID 32359397, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2020.04.002); Msaouel et al., *Mol Cell Oncol* 2020, PMID 32944636, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/23723556.2020.1777060)).

**cGAS-STING innate immune activation.** The focal copy-number alterations in RMC associate with activation of the cGAS-STING innate immune pathway, giving RMC a highly inflamed phenotype. This finding initially suggested that immune checkpoint therapy might be effective, though prospective trials have not borne this out for single-agent checkpoint blockade (see Sections 3 and 5) (Msaouel et al. 2020, PMID 32359397, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2020.04.002)).

**EZH2 dependency (the SWI/SNF-to-PRC2 axis).** Because the SWI/SNF (BAF) complex and the EZH2-containing PRC2 complex have antagonistic activities on chromatin, loss of SWI/SNF function (via SMARCB1 loss) shifts the balance toward EZH2-mediated repression, creating an oncogenic dependence on EZH2. This dependency was first established in SMARCB1-deficient rhabdoid tumors and extends across the SWI/SNF-deficient family (Chan-Penebre et al., *Mol Cancer Ther* 2017, PMID 28292935, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1158/1535-7163.MCT-16-0678)). In RMC specifically, patient-derived cell lines (UOK353, UOK360) overexpress EZH2 and other polycomb repressive complex members, and EZH2 inhibition reduced RMC spheroid viability (Wei et al. 2020, PMID 32259323, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/gcc.22847)). This is the mechanistic basis for the flagship tazemetostat translation (Section 5).

**Microsatellite stability / low TMB.** RMC has a quiet coding genome with no recurrent point mutations (Carlo et al. 2017, PMID 28558987, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clgc.2017.04.012)) and is driven by copy-number instability and replication stress rather than by hypermutation (Msaouel et al. 2020, PMID 32359397, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2020.04.002)). RMC is therefore generally microsatellite stable with low tumor mutational burden. **Consequence:** the tissue-agnostic MSI-H and TMB-high checkpoint approvals (pembrolizumab 2017/2020) essentially do NOT apply to RMC. Note: I did not find a dedicated RMC MSI/TMB prevalence study, so the specific quantitative MSI-H/TMB-high frequency in RMC is **UNVERIFIED**, but the MSS/low-TMB biology is strongly supported by the genomic profiling above.

**Bottom line.** RMC = biallelic SMARCB1/INI1 loss (near-sole driver) -> residual SWI/SNF imbalance creating EZH2 dependency + MYC-driven replication stress + cGAS-STING innate immune activation, on an otherwise MSS/low-TMB genome.

## 3. Standard of Care

RMC has no FDA-approved therapy and no established standard beyond platinum-based cytotoxic chemotherapy borrowed from other settings. Because of its rarity, many decisions rest on case reports and small series (Wei et al. 2020, PMID 32259323, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/gcc.22847); Beckermann et al. 2017, PMID 28697319, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1200/JOP.2017.020909)).

**Platinum-based cytotoxic chemotherapy (first-line).** Platinum-based regimens (e.g., cisplatin or carboplatin with gemcitabine and/or paclitaxel) are the preferred systemic treatment. In the pooled analysis, patients receiving platinum-paclitaxel-gemcitabine had longer disease control than those given topoisomerase inhibitors or targeted therapies, and platinum-based chemotherapy was concluded to be the preferred systemic option (Iacovelli et al. 2015, PMID 26085875, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5489/cuaj.2373)). Durability is nonetheless poor: median progression-free survival on platinum was 2.5 months in the MSKCC series (Carlo et al. 2017, PMID 28558987, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clgc.2017.04.012)).

**Targeted agents used in other RCC subtypes are ineffective.** RMC is resistant to the VEGF-pathway and mTOR inhibitors used in clear cell RCC; these are ineffective as monotherapy (Beckermann et al. 2017, PMID 28697319, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1200/JOP.2017.020909); Msaouel et al. 2020, PMID 32359397, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2020.04.002)). Guidance for non-clear cell RCC likewise reserves platinum-based chemotherapy for collecting duct and medullary RCC (Drobner et al., *J Kidney Cancer VHL* 2023, PMID 37789902, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.15586/jkcvhl.v10i3.295)).

**Bevacizumab added to chemotherapy: no benefit.** The prospective phase II BEVABEL-GETUG/AFU24 trial added bevacizumab to gemcitabine plus platinum in metastatic RMC/collecting duct carcinoma. It was closed early for toxicity and lack of efficacy: median overall survival was 11.1 months, 6-month objective response rate 29.4%, grade 3 to 4 toxicity in 82%, and the authors concluded gemcitabine-platinum without bevacizumab remains the option (Thibault et al., *Eur J Cancer* 2023, PMID 37054556, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ejca.2023.03.018)).

**Surgery.** Radical nephrectomy as an up-front strategy did not confer a survival benefit in the pooled analysis but may be used to palliate local symptoms or establish diagnosis (Iacovelli et al. 2015, PMID 26085875, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5489/cuaj.2373)).

**Unmet need.** Even with platinum, metastatic RMC survival rarely extends beyond ~1 year, motivating the cross-cancer translations in Section 5.

## 5. Cross-Cancer Translatable Therapies (PRIORITY SECTION)

**Evidence tiers.**
- **Tier 1** = FDA tissue-agnostic approval applicable if the biomarker is present.
- **Tier 2** = approved in another tumor type with the SAME target/biology present in RMC, OR a positive prospective/basket signal specifically in RMC.
- **Tier 3** = mechanistic/preclinical rationale only, no positive clinical RMC data (or a negative/mixed early clinical signal).

### Flagship translation: EZH2 inhibition (tazemetostat)

**Tazemetostat (EZH2 inhibitor), Tier 3 for RMC (honest grade).** Tazemetostat is FDA-approved (accelerated, January 23, 2020) as the first EZH2 inhibitor, for INI1/SMARCB1-deficient advanced epithelioid sarcoma not eligible for complete resection (ORR 15% in the pivotal cohort of 62; Gounder et al., *Lancet Oncol* 2020, PMID 33035459, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1470-2045(20)30451-4)); it is also approved in EZH2-mutant relapsed/refractory follicular lymphoma. The scientific rationale is compelling: RMC shares the SWI/SNF-loss-driven EZH2 dependency of the rhabdoid family (Chan-Penebre PMID 28292935, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1158/1535-7163.MCT-16-0678); Wei PMID 32259323, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/gcc.22847)). **However, RMC-specific clinical evidence is weak.** In the dedicated tazemetostat phase II basket study (NCT02601950), the renal medullary carcinoma cohort (Cohort 4, n=14) had a 0% objective response rate and did not meet its efficacy threshold, while responses were seen in malignant rhabdoid tumors (9%) and INI1-negative tumors (9%) (Gounder et al., *Nat Commun* 2026, PMID 41882006, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-69708-2)). In NCI-COG Pediatric MATCH (APEC1621C), one RMC patient had a best response of stable disease (Chi et al., *J Natl Cancer Inst* 2023, PMID 37228094, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1093/jnci/djad085)). A cautionary case documented glial transdifferentiation in an RMC brain metastasis after tazemetostat, suggesting epigenetic-modulator-driven plasticity (Gubbiotti et al. 2025, PMID 39833894, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1186/s40478-025-01929-w)). **Verdict: strong mechanistic rationale but negative single-agent RMC signal; Tier 3. The authors of the basket study explicitly call for combination strategies when EZH2 is not the sole driver.**

### Proteasome inhibition (bortezomib)

**Bortezomib (proteasome inhibitor, borrowed from multiple myeloma), Tier 2 for RMC.** Bortezomib emerged from an unbiased high-throughput drug screen of RMC cell lines as having in vitro and in vivo antitumor activity, partly dependent on increased oxidative stress, and it synergized with cisplatin (Wei et al. 2020, PMID 32259323, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/gcc.22847)). Clinically, two pediatric patients with metastatic RMC treated with platinum-based chemotherapy plus bortezomib achieved complete responses, with one alive 7 years and one disease-free nearly 2 years from diagnosis (Carden et al., *Pediatr Blood Cancer* 2017, PMID 28052556, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/pbc.26402)). **Verdict: preclinical activity plus durable complete responses in a small case series with a platinum backbone; Tier 2 (repurposed from myeloma).**

### Immune checkpoint blockade (PD-1/PD-L1)

**Pembrolizumab / anti-PD-1 monotherapy, Tier 3 (negative prospective signal).** Despite the inflamed cGAS-STING phenotype, the first prospective evaluation of a checkpoint inhibitor in RMC (pembrolizumab, phase II basket NCT02721732, n=5) showed no clinical activity: all patients had rapid progression (median time to progression 8.7 weeks) regardless of PD-L1 or tumor-infiltrating-lymphocyte levels (Nze et al., *Cancers* 2023, PMID 37568622, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.3390/cancers15153806)). A single nivolumab case report described a response, but this is anecdotal (Di Stefano et al., *Clin Genitourin Cancer* 2018, PMID 30122516, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clgc.2018.07.011)). **Because RMC is MSS/low-TMB, the tissue-agnostic MSI-H (2017) and TMB-high (2020) pembrolizumab approvals do NOT apply.** Combination checkpoint strategies remain under investigation (Section 6). **Verdict: Tier 3 for single-agent PD-1; not supported by the tissue-agnostic MSI/TMB labels.**

### Replication-stress / DNA-damage-repair targeting

**Agents targeting replication stress and DNA-damage repair (e.g., ATR, PARP, MYC-associated vulnerabilities), Tier 3 (mechanistic).** RMC's high MYC-driven replication stress was shown to be a therapeutic vulnerability to DNA-damage-repair-directed drugs in the primary profiling study (Msaouel et al., *Cancer Cell* 2020, PMID 32359397, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2020.04.002); commentary PMID 32944636, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/23723556.2020.1777060)). No specific ATR/PARP inhibitor has an approved RMC indication and I did not find a positive RMC clinical trial readout for these agents, so this remains a mechanistic Tier 3 direction. This is also the biological rationale behind combining bortezomib and platinum with cytotoxic/DNA-damaging backbones.

## 7. Tissue-Agnostic Precedent Primer

The following landmark tumor-agnostic FDA approvals establish the biomarker-driven paradigm. **The intellectually honest point for RMC: because RMC is MSS/low-TMB with a quiet coding genome and no recurrent NTRK/BRAF/RET/HER2 alterations reported, most of these do NOT apply.** They are included as the conceptual backbone and to make explicit which are inapplicable.

| Biomarker | Drug | Year | Applies to RMC? | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| MSI-H / dMMR | pembrolizumab | 2017 (first tissue-agnostic approval) | **No**, RMC is MSS/low-TMB | KEYNOTE-158, Maio/Marabelle et al., *Ann Oncol* 2022, PMID 35680043, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.annonc.2022.05.519) |
| TMB-high (>=10 mut/Mb) | pembrolizumab | 2020 | **No**, RMC has low TMB | KEYNOTE-158 (TMB analysis); MSS/low-TMB biology per Msaouel PMID 32359397, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ccell.2020.04.002) |
| dMMR (recurrent/advanced) | dostarlimab | 2021 | **No**, RMC is MMR-proficient | GARNET dMMR ORR ~41.6%; genomic basis per Carlo PMID 28558987, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clgc.2017.04.012) |
| NTRK fusion | larotrectinib | 2018 (tissue-agnostic) | **Only if a rare NTRK fusion is documented (not reported in RMC; UNVERIFIED)** | Hong et al., *Lancet Oncol* 2020, PMID 32105622, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1470-2045(19)30856-3) |
| NTRK fusion | entrectinib | 2019 (tissue-agnostic) | **Only if NTRK fusion documented (UNVERIFIED in RMC)** | FDA tissue-agnostic approval 2019 |
| BRAF V600E | dabrafenib + trametinib | 2022 (tissue-agnostic, non-CRC) | **No**, BRAF V600E not a feature of RMC | FDA tissue-agnostic approval 2022 |
| RET fusion | selpercatinib | 2022 (tissue-agnostic) | **Only if a RET fusion documented (not reported in RMC; UNVERIFIED)** | FDA tissue-agnostic approval 2022 |
| HER2 IHC 3+ | trastuzumab deruxtecan | 2024 (tissue-agnostic) | **Only if HER2 IHC 3+ documented (UNVERIFIED in RMC)** | FDA tissue-agnostic approval 2024 |

The RMC-relevant lesson from this paradigm is that RMC's actionable biomarker is not a hypermutation phenotype or a kinase fusion, but the SWI/SNF/SMARCB1 loss that defines it. The nearest "biomarker-to-drug" precedent for RMC is the SMARCB1-loss -> EZH2-inhibitor logic proven in epithelioid sarcoma (Gounder *Lancet Oncol* 2020, PMID 33035459, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1470-2045(20)30451-4)), even though single-agent tazemetostat underperformed in the RMC cohort itself (PMID 41882006, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-69708-2)).

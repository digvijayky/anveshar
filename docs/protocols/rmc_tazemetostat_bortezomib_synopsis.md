# Phase 1b/2 protocol synopsis: tazemetostat plus bortezomib in SMARCB1 deficient renal medullary carcinoma

## 1. Background and rationale

Renal medullary carcinoma (RMC) is an ultra rare, aggressive kidney cancer of young patients with sickle cell trait, defined by complete biallelic loss of SMARCB1 (INI1). Median overall survival in metastatic disease is roughly 4 to 13 months and there is no approved targeted therapy (Msaouel et al., Cancer Cell 2020, DOI 10.1016/j.ccell.2020.04.002).

Two independent, individually incomplete signals motivate the combination:

1. EZH2 axis. Loss of the SWI/SNF subunit SMARCB1 shifts chromatin control to the PRC2/EZH2 complex, an established rhabdoid family dependency. The EZH2 inhibitor tazemetostat is FDA approved in INI1 negative epithelioid sarcoma, but its dedicated RMC cohort produced 0 of 14 objective responses and pediatric MATCH showed only stable disease in an RMC patient (Chi et al., J Natl Cancer Inst 2023, DOI 10.1093/jnci/djad085; Gounder et al., Nat Commun 2026, DOI 10.1038/s41467-026-69708-2). A case report documents EZH2 inhibitor driven transdifferentiation as an adaptive escape (Gubbiotti et al., Acta Neuropathol Commun 2025, DOI 10.1186/s40478-025-01929-w).

2. Proteasome axis. Functional studies show RMC survival depends on SMARCB1 loss and confers sensitivity to proteasome inhibition, and an independent RMC cell line drug screen nominated bortezomib with cisplatin synergy (Hong et al., eLife 2019, DOI 10.7554/eLife.44161; Wei et al., Genes Chromosomes Cancer 2020, PMID 32259323). A platinum plus bortezomib backbone produced durable complete responses in a small pediatric RMC series (Carden et al., Pediatr Blood Cancer 2017, PMID 28052556).

Rationale for combination: single agent EZH2 inhibition is inactive in RMC and single agent immune checkpoint blockade also failed in RMC despite an inflamed phenotype, so a second, orthogonal proteotoxic hit (bortezomib) is proposed to convert the EZH2 dependency into a lethal one and to blunt the epigenetic escape observed on tazemetostat monotherapy.

## 3. Objectives

Primary. Phase 1b: determine the recommended phase 2 dose and safety of tazemetostat plus bortezomib. Phase 2: estimate objective response rate (RECIST 1.1).

Secondary. Progression free survival, overall survival, duration of response, disease control rate, safety and tolerability.

Exploratory. On treatment change in H3K27me3 and SMARCB1 status, proteasome pathway and NRF2 oxidative stress signatures, and whether transdifferentiation markers emerge, to test the escape hypothesis.

## 4. Design

Open label, single arm, multicenter Phase 1b/2. Phase 1b uses a 3 plus 3 or Bayesian optimal interval dose escalation of bortezomib on a fixed approved tazemetostat backbone. Phase 2 is a Simon two stage expansion at the recommended phase 2 dose. Given RMC rarity, allow adults and adolescents (age 12 and older) and permit enrollment of other SMARCB1 deficient tumors as a signal seeking secondary cohort.

## 5. Eligibility (summary)

Key inclusion: histologically confirmed RMC with loss of SMARCB1/INI1 by immunohistochemistry; measurable disease by RECIST 1.1; prior platinum based therapy allowed and expected; ECOG 0 to 1 (or Lansky/Karnofsky equivalent); adequate organ and marrow function.

Key exclusion: baseline peripheral neuropathy grade 2 or higher (bortezomib risk); strong CYP3A inhibitors or inducers within the washout window (tazemetostat is a CYP3A substrate; see DDI plan); prior EZH2 inhibitor with documented progression is a stratification factor, not necessarily exclusion; uncontrolled intercurrent illness.

## 6. Intervention and dosing

Tazemetostat 800 mg orally twice daily continuously (approved epithelioid sarcoma dose), 28 day cycles.

Bortezomib by subcutaneous injection on a myeloma style schedule (for example days 1, 4, 8, 11 of a 21 day block, or a weekly days 1, 8, 15 schedule to improve tolerability), starting dose and final schedule set by the phase 1b escalation. Subcutaneous administration is preferred to reduce neuropathy.

Drug interaction plan. Because tazemetostat is a CYP3A substrate and bortezomib is metabolized by CYP3A4 and CYP2C19, phase 1b includes a limited pharmacokinetic run in to confirm the combination does not materially alter tazemetostat exposure, mitigating overlap with the findings of the EZH-105 interaction study.

## 7. Endpoints

Primary phase 1b: dose limiting toxicity and recommended phase 2 dose. Primary phase 2: objective response rate by RECIST 1.1, independently reviewed.

Secondary: progression free survival, overall survival, duration of response, disease control rate at 12 weeks, safety by CTCAE v5.

## 8. Statistics

Phase 2 Simon two stage design testing a null ORR of 5 percent (consistent with single agent tazemetostat 0 of 14 and low platinum ORR) against an alternative of 25 percent, one sided alpha 0.10, power 0.80. ## 9. Safety oversight

Continuous adverse event monitoring by CTCAE v5, protocol defined dose modification rules for thrombocytopenia, neuropathy, and gastrointestinal toxicity, and an independent data safety monitoring board given the pediatric adolescent inclusion and combination toxicity risk. Prespecified stopping rules for excess grade 3 or higher toxicity.
